# Loom Walkthrough Script — Sandler Partner Onboarding

**Duration:** ~8 minutes
**Purpose:** Sent before discovery call. Answers "what is this?" so the call can focus on THEIR practice.
**Tone:** Peer. Sandler-insider. No pitch energy.

---

## Opening (0:00-0:45)

[Screen: your face, casual background]

"Hey — John Cunningham here. You booked a call to talk about the reinforcement diagnostics layer. Before we get on the phone, I want to show you what it actually looks like in 8 minutes — so our time together is about your practice, not my product demo."

"What I'm about to show you is a scored sales call, run through the Sandler framework, with feedback that lands in the rep's inbox within an hour of hanging up. Your brand on it. Your client sees your logo. I'm invisible."

---

## The Problem (0:45-1:30)

[Screen: simple text slide — "Training → 60 Days → ?"]

"You know this story. Rep gets trained. Up-Front Contract, Pain Funnel, Dummy Curve — all of it. Two months later, on a live call, the U-F-C gets soft. The Pain Funnel gets skipped. Nobody notices. The methodology erodes."

"Why? Not because the rep is lazy. Not because the training was bad. Because there's nothing between the workshop and the next call except memory and pressure. No reinforcement loop."

---

## What It Looks Like (1:30-4:30)

[Screen: OCC dashboard — a scored call]

"This is what we built. Every call your client's reps make gets scored against the Sandler framework automatically."

[Walk through the scored call elements]

"The system looks for specific Sandler components. Was the Up-Front Contract set? Did they run the Pain Funnel? Did they handle the Dummy Curve or did they take it? Did they isolate the budget before proposing?"

"It scores each component. Green means executed. Yellow means attempted but incomplete. Red means missed entirely."

"The rep gets this within an hour of the call. Not 'good job' or 'work on your close.' Specific: 'You skipped the Pain Funnel at 4:12 when the prospect said they're exploring options. Next time, ask what exploring means specifically.'"

"That's the feedback loop. Every call. Not two calls per rep. Not manager spot checks. Every call."

---

## What the Client Sees (4:30-5:30)

[Screen: partner-branded dashboard mockup]

"Here's the important part. This is white-labeled. Your logo. Your brand colors. Your client logs in and sees YOUR Sandler practice delivering this."

"They don't know I exist. You install the operating system. I'm just the diagnostics layer running underneath."

"The client dashboard shows: overall methodology adherence, per-rep scores over time, specific weakness patterns across the team. The data you need to have the renewal conversation before it happens."

---

## The Pilot Offer (5:30-6:30)

[Screen: simple text — "30 Days. Your Brand. No Cost."]

"Here's the offer. One of your clients, 30 days, white-labeled, no cost to you or them."

"If it produces nothing useful, it cost you nothing. If it does — you'll have a case study in your own client's numbers. Rep-specific data. Before and after. Methodology adherence over time."

"No slides. No pitch deck. Scored calls. That's it."

---

## What I Need From You (6:30-7:15)

[Screen: simple checklist]

"On our call, I'll ask you two things:

One: which client would be the best pilot? Someone who trusts you, runs a 5-15 rep team, uses Sandler actively.

Two: which 3-5 Sandler components matter most to you? Every franchisee has their non-negotiables. We'll score to yours."

"That's it. 30 days. Your brand. Real data."

---

## Close (7:15-8:00)

[Screen: your face]

"On our call, we won't do any of this again. You just saw it. We'll talk about YOUR practice — your clients, your bottlenecks, your renewals."

"If this doesn't fit, no problem. But if the reinforcement gap has been eating at you for years — and I know it has — this closes it."

"See you on the call."

---

## Production Notes

- No slides with bullet points. Single words or short phrases only.
- When showing the dashboard, move slowly. Let it breathe.
- The "I'm invisible" line matters. Say it like you mean it.
- Record at 1x speed. Don't rush.
- Test the audio before recording the full take.
