# LinkedIn Post Draft — Training Abandonment (Round 1)

**Date:** 2026-05-20  
**Pillar:** Story + Insight / Thought Leadership  
**Audience:** Sales trainers, Sandler franchisees, methodology practitioners  
**Round:** 1 (names the problem, no product mention)

---

Most sales training doesn't fail.

It gets abandoned.

I've spent enough time around sales trainers to see the pattern clearly. A team goes through Sandler, or Challenger, or MEDDIC. For about three weeks, the methodology shows up in conversations. Language shifts. Questions improve.

Then pipeline pressure returns.

The manager who was supposed to reinforce goes back to deal reviews. The rep who started strong reverts to what feels natural under stress. Within 90 days, the training investment is invisible — not because the training was weak, but because nothing was built to sustain it.

The trainers I respect most carry this quietly. They deliver excellent work, then watch it erode inside organizations that treat training as an event rather than the start of a behavior-change process.

The industry talks endlessly about better training. Very few talk about what happens after.

If you're a trainer who's watched your work fade inside a client's organization, I see it. And I think it's the silent problem in sales development that nobody's solving.
