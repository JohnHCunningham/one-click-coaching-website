# LSL Group Post: The Perception Gap
## Draft — June 10, 2026

I keep seeing the same stat pair and it's bothering me.

83% of sales leaders say they're great at coaching.
48% of reps say they never get any.

Same building. Opposite stories. Nobody's lying — there's just no record. The manager genuinely believes that pipeline review is coaching. The rep genuinely experiences it as an interrogation.

Question for this group: what's the real number in your org? Not what you aspire to. Not what the training manual says. When was the last time you asked your reps to rate your coaching — anonymously?

I'm asking because I think this gap is wider than anyone admits. And because the math gets worse: 71% of managers were never trained to coach. We're asking untrained people to develop other people, and then we're surprised when it doesn't happen.
