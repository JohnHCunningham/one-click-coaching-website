Most Challenger training follows the same arc.

Week 1: Reps are teaching for differentiation. They're tailoring the message to mobilizers. They're taking control of the conversation.

Week 4: The teaching sounds more like presenting. The tailoring has become templating. Control has softened into accommodation.

Week 12: Commercial teaching is gone. They're back to positioning features. The mobilizer framework is a deck they don't open anymore.

The problem isn't the Challenger methodology. It's that nobody reinforced it after the training ended.

Reinforcement is the difference between a rep who can describe Teach-Tailor-Take Control and one who executes it on a live call.

For those who've been through Challenger training — how long did the methodology actually hold before old habits crept back in?
