TITLE: The Pipeline Mirage

HOOK: Your CRM shows deals advancing. Your call recordings show qualification never happened. One of these is the truth.

CONTEXT: Monday morning pipeline review. The deck is up. Deals move through stages. Heads nod. Then you pull one recording. The deal in "proposal" never had a budget conversation. The rep never confirmed the decision process. The framework your team was trained on is nowhere. The CRM says the deal moved. The call says it shouldn't have.

INSIGHT: CRM stages measure what a rep clicked — not what they executed. A deal can advance through five stages skipping every qualification behavior that makes a deal real. The CRM accepts the stage change regardless. It updates the probability and colors the dashboard green. The methodology disappears from the field without anyone noticing. The costs compound: forecast accuracy, pipeline integrity, and the quiet moment when the fourth rep realizes methodology is optional.

CLOSE: Pipeline inspection should start with a different question. Not "is the deal advancing?" — "is the deal advancing because the methodology was executed?"
