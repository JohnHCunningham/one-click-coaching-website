# Sandler Group Post: The Dummy Curve
## Draft — June 10, 2026

You know the curve. Every franchisee knows it.

A rep adopts the Sandler methodology. For the first few calls, it feels foreign. They stumble. Their performance dips. They panic.

And here's where it breaks: they revert. They go back to feature-dumping and discounting because that's what feels safe. The training dies right there — in the dip, before the recovery.

I've been calling this DRIFT — methodology decay that starts the moment the trainer leaves the room. But a Sandler client described it as the Dummy Curve, and I think that language is sharper. The rep isn't failing. They're in the abandonment window where everything feels harder before it gets better.

What do you tell your franchisee clients about holding a rep through that window? Because if they can't hold them through the dip, the training was a donation.
