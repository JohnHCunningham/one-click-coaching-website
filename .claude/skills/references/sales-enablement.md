# Sales Enablement — One Click Coaching

**Purpose:** Qualify prospects fast, run discovery calls with precision, demo with clarity, close deals with confidence.

**Principle:** Discovery before demo. Qualification before pitch. Insight before solution.

---

## Discovery Call Structure (45 Minutes)

**Goal:** Qualify or disqualify in first 15 minutes. If qualified, uncover pain and book demo. If not qualified, end gracefully.

---

### Pre-Call Prep (5 Minutes)

**Research checklist:**
- [ ] Review LinkedIn profile (role, tenure, recent posts)
- [ ] Check company website (team size, methodology mentioned, recent news)
- [ ] Scan CRM notes (how did they hear about OCC? What email template worked?)
- [ ] Review any inbound signals (which blog post did they read? What LinkedIn post did they comment on?)

**Hypothesis before call:**
- What's their likely pain? (methodology drift, manual coaching doesn't scale, Gong gap)
- Are they ICP? (5-25 reps, defined methodology, $5M+ revenue)
- What's the buying trigger? (recent training rollout, new manager, performance plateau)

---

### Phase 1: Rapport + Agenda (Minutes 0-3)

**Opening:**
> "Thanks for making time. I know you're busy, so here's how I'd like to use the next 45 minutes: I'll ask you a few questions to understand what you're working on, you can ask me about OCC, and if it makes sense to go deeper, I'll show you what the platform looks like. Sound good?"

**Key move:** Set the agenda. Take control. Show respect for their time.

**Avoid:**
- Small talk beyond 60 seconds
- Pitching before discovery
- "Tell me about yourself"

---

### Phase 2: Qualification (Minutes 3-15)

**Goal:** Disqualify fast if not ICP. If qualified, move to Phase 3.

**The 5 Qualification Questions (Ask in Order):**

#### Q1: "What sales methodology is your team trained on?"

**Listen for:**
- ✓ Sandler → Perfect fit, proceed.
- ✓ MEDDIC, SPIN, Challenger → "Those are on our roadmap but not live yet. Let me know if you want to revisit in 6 months."
- ✗ "We don't have a defined methodology." → Disqualify: "OCC reinforces methodology. Without one, it won't help. Let's revisit once you've installed a framework."

**Disqualify immediately if no methodology.** Do not proceed.

#### Q2: "How many reps do you have?"

**Listen for:**
- ✓ 5-25 reps → ICP range, proceed.
- ✗ < 5 reps → "OCC is built for teams of 5+. Let's revisit when you scale."
- ✗ > 50 reps → Confirm budget authority. Enterprise pricing required.

**If < 5 reps, end call gracefully.** No point proceeding.

#### Q3: "What's the pain you're trying to solve?"

**Listen for buying signals:**
- ✓ "Reps revert to old habits under pressure" → Methodology drift. Core pain.
- ✓ "Training ROI fades after 90 days" → Reinforcement gap. Core pain.
- ✓ "Managers don't have time to coach every call" → Capacity problem. Core pain.
- ✓ "We use Gong but reps aren't changing behavior" → Feedback loop gap. Core pain.

**Listen for disqualifying signals:**
- ✗ "We need call recording" → Wrong tool. Recommend Gong/Chorus.
- ✗ "We want AI to replace managers" → Wrong expectation. End call.
- ✗ "We need this to work with [unsupported tool]" → Integration gap. Disqualify or defer.

**If pain doesn't match OCC's solution, disqualify.**

#### Q4: "When did you invest in [Sandler/MEDDIC/etc.] training?"

**Listen for:**
- ✓ Within last 12 months → Fresh pain. High urgency.
- ✓ 1-2 years ago → ROI erosion visible. Medium urgency.
- ✗ 3+ years ago or never → Stale. Low urgency.

**If 3+ years ago, ask:** "What's prompting you to revisit this now?" (Look for trigger event.)

#### Q5: "What does success look like in 6 months?"

**Listen for realistic expectations:**
- ✓ "Reps consistently execute the methodology" → Aligned. Proceed.
- ✓ "Training ROI measurable and sustained" → Aligned. Proceed.
- ✗ "AI replaces all manager coaching" → Misaligned. Clarify or disqualify.
- ✗ "10x revenue growth" → Unrealistic. Reset expectations or disqualify.

**If expectations are unrealistic, reset or end call.**

---

### Disqualification Exit Script

**If disqualified in Phase 2:**
> "Based on what you've shared, I don't think OCC is the right fit right now. [Specific reason: no methodology, team too small, pain doesn't match, etc.] I'd rather be honest than waste your time. If [condition changes], let's reconnect. Cool?"

**Key move:** End gracefully. Preserve relationship. Don't burn bridges.

---

### Phase 3: Pain Excavation (Minutes 15-30)

**Goal:** If qualified, dig deeper into pain. Quantify impact. Build urgency.

**The Pain Funnel (Sandler-Style):**

#### Level 1: Surface Pain
> "You mentioned reps revert to old habits. Can you give me an example?"

**Listen for:** Specific story. Names. Deals lost.

#### Level 2: Impact
> "How is that affecting the team's performance?"

**Listen for:** Pipeline stalls, longer sales cycles, lower close rates, rep turnover.

#### Level 3: Quantify
> "What's that costing you? In revenue, in manager time, in training budget?"

**Listen for:** Dollar amounts. Hours per week. Deals lost per quarter.

**Example:**
- Lost deals: 5 per quarter at $50k ACV = $250k/year
- Manager time: 10 hours/week on manual coaching = $50k/year opportunity cost
- Training budget: $200k investment with < 20% ROI = $160k waste

**Your job:** Make the pain concrete. Numbers, not vibes.

#### Level 4: Consequence
> "If this continues for another 6 months, what happens?"

**Listen for:** Fear. Urgency. Consequences (missed quota, team morale drops, manager burnout).

#### Level 5: Commitment to Change
> "What have you tried so far?"

**Listen for:** Past attempts. Why they failed. What's different now.

**Then:**
> "If I can show you a system that solves this, are you ready to move on it?"

**Listen for:** "Yes" (qualified) or "I need to think about it" (not urgent, nurture).

---

### Phase 4: Demo Tee-Up (Minutes 30-35)

**Transition:**
> "Based on what you've shared, I think OCC can help. Let me show you exactly how it works. I'll walk through a real call score so you can see what your reps would receive. Sound good?"

**Key move:** Anchor the demo in *their* pain. Not a generic demo. A solution to *their* problem.

---

### Phase 5: Micro-Demo (Minutes 35-43)

**Goal:** Show, don't tell. Use real example. Tie to their pain.

**Screen share: OCC platform**

#### Step 1: Show Call Score (2 min)
> "Here's a scored call. This rep was trained on Sandler. The call got an overall score of 6.2 out of 10. Let me show you why."

**Point out:**
- 8 components scored (Pain Funnel, Budget, Decision, etc.)
- Each component has a rubric (what good looks like)
- Specific timestamps where rep succeeded or missed

**Tie to their pain:**
> "Remember you said your reps skip the Pain Funnel under pressure? This is how we catch that — same day the call happened."

#### Step 2: Show Coaching Output (2 min)
> "Based on this score, here's the coaching that fires to the rep."

**Point out:**
- Coaching is specific (not generic "ask better questions")
- Grounded in methodology (Sandler Pain Funnel framework)
- Includes examples (what good sounds like)
- Manager approves before sending (human in the loop)

**Tie to their pain:**
> "Your managers don't have time to listen to every call. This automates the basics so they coach exceptions, not fundamentals."

#### Step 3: Show Non-Repetition Engine (1 min)
> "Here's where it gets interesting. The system tracks what this rep has been coached on. If they've already received Pain Funnel feedback twice, we won't send it again. Instead, we escalate to the manager for 1:1 intervention."

**Point out:**
- Coaching evolves with the rep
- No spam, no repetition
- Escalation logic (3 strikes → manager notification)

**Tie to their pain:**
> "You said training fades after 90 days. This is how we prevent that — continuous reinforcement, not one-time feedback."

#### Step 4: Show Manager Dashboard (1 min)
> "Managers see all pending coaching in one view. They approve, edit, or kill it. Once approved, coaching fires via email or Slack."

**Point out:**
- Manager stays in control
- Can edit coaching before sending
- Can see patterns across team (which components are weakest)

**Tie to their pain:**
> "This gives your managers visibility they don't have today — without listening to 50 calls a week."

#### Step 5: White-Label View (1 min)
> "One more thing — this is white-label ready. Your logo, your colors, your company name. Looks like internal tooling, not third-party software."

**Why this matters:** Reps trust feedback that looks like it's from their company, not random AI.

---

### Phase 6: Trial Close + Next Steps (Minutes 43-45)

**Ask:**
> "Based on what you've seen, does this solve the problem you described?"

**Listen for:**
- ✓ "Yes, this is exactly what we need." → Move to pricing.
- ✓ "This could work, but I need to talk to [stakeholder]." → Book follow-up with stakeholder.
- ✗ "I'm not sure this is the right fit." → Surface objection, handle, or disqualify.

**If yes, move to pricing:**
> "Let's talk about what this looks like for your team. You said you have [X] reps. That puts you in our [Tier] plan at $[Price]/month. How does that compare to what you were expecting?"

**Listen for:**
- ✓ "That's in range." → Move to contract.
- ✗ "That's higher than expected." → Handle pricing objection (see below).

**Next steps:**
> "Here's what I'd suggest: I'll send over a proposal with pricing and next steps. You review it with [stakeholder]. We'll book a follow-up call to finalize. Does [date] work?"

**Key move:** Always book the next meeting before ending this call.

### Pricing Objection Handling

#### Objection: "That's more than we budgeted."

**Response:**
> "I get it. Let's break it down. You said you're losing [X deals per quarter] at [$Y ACV] because reps aren't executing the methodology. That's [$Z] in lost revenue per year. OCC costs [$Price/year]. If we prevent even 2 of those lost deals, the platform pays for itself 5x over. Make sense?"

**Key move:** ROI reframe. Compare cost to cost of inaction.

#### Objection: "Can we start with a smaller plan?"

**Response:**
> "Absolutely. Starter plan covers up to 10 reps at $249/month. You can scale up as you see results. Want to start there?"

**Key move:** Remove friction. Let them start small.

#### Objection: "We need to see results before committing."

**Response:**
> "Totally fair. Here's what I'd suggest: 30-day pilot with 5 reps. If the coaching quality isn't there or reps aren't using it, you walk away. If it works, we expand to the full team. Sound reasonable?"

**Key move:** Pilot reduces risk.

## Objection Handling (Common Blockers)

### Objection: "We need buy-in from [stakeholder]."

**Response:**
> "Makes sense. Who needs to be involved in this decision?"

**Then:**
> "Let's book a follow-up call with them. I'll walk through exactly what I showed you today. Does [date] work for the three of us?"

**Key move:** Get the stakeholder on the next call. Don't let this drift.

### Objection: "We need to evaluate other options."

**Response:**
> "Smart move. What other tools are you looking at?"

**Listen for:** Gong, Allego, generic AI coaching, etc.

**Then:**
> "Happy to walk through how OCC compares. The main difference is [specific differentiator — e.g., methodology-specific scoring, non-repetition engine, manager approval gate]. Want me to send over a comparison doc?"

**Key move:** Position against competitors without trashing them.

### Objection: "We're not sure our team will use it."

**Response:**
> "That's the right concern. Adoption is everything. Here's how we de-risk it: manager approval gate means reps only get feedback their manager vouches for. That builds trust. Over time, reps see the coaching is accurate and start self-correcting before the manager even sees it. We've seen 80%+ engagement within 60 days when managers are involved from day one."

**Key move:** Emphasize manager approval as trust mechanism.

### Objection: "Can we start with a free trial?"

**Response:**
> "We don't do free trials because setup requires integration with your CRM and call recording tool — that's real work on both sides. Instead, we do a 30-day pilot at the Starter plan rate. If it doesn't work, you're out $249 and we part as friends. If it works, we expand. That work for you?"

**Key move:** Paid pilot > free trial. Shows commitment.

## Qualification Signals (Green Lights)

**Strong buy signals:**
- [ ] Prospect has budget authority (VP of Sales, CRO, founder)
- [ ] Recent methodology training rollout (within 12 months)
- [ ] Quantified pain (lost deals, wasted training budget, manager burnout)
- [ ] Urgency trigger (quota pressure, new manager, board scrutiny)
- [ ] Already tried manual coaching or other tools (knows the gap)
- [ ] Uses Gong/Chorus (understands conversation intelligence, sees the gap)

**If 4+ signals = hot lead. Move fast.**

---

## Disqualification Signals (Red Flags)

**Hard disqualifiers:**
- [ ] No defined methodology (nothing to reinforce)
- [ ] Team < 5 reps (too small)
- [ ] No budget authority (can't make decision)
- [ ] Wants AI to replace managers (wrong expectation)
- [ ] Needs integration we don't support (technical blocker)
- [ ] No urgency (exploring, not buying)

**If 2+ disqualifiers = nurture or disqualify. Don't waste time.**

## Demo Flow Checklist

**Pre-demo:**
- [ ] Qualified via 5 questions (methodology, team size, pain, timeline, success criteria)
- [ ] Excavated pain (specific stories, quantified impact)
- [ ] Confirmed decision-maker on call (or booked follow-up with them)

**During demo:**
- [ ] Showed call score with real example
- [ ] Tied scoring to their specific pain
- [ ] Showed coaching output (methodology-grounded, specific)
- [ ] Explained non-repetition engine + escalation logic
- [ ] Showed manager approval workflow
- [ ] Highlighted white-label capability

**Post-demo:**
- [ ] Asked trial close question ("Does this solve your problem?")
- [ ] Discussed pricing (transparent, tied to ROI)
- [ ] Handled objections (if any surfaced)
- [ ] Booked next step (follow-up call, stakeholder demo, contract review)
- [ ] Sent follow-up email within 2 hours (proposal, pricing, calendar link)

## Pipeline Stages

**Track every deal through these stages:**

1. **Discovery Scheduled** — Meeting booked, not yet held
2. **Discovery Completed** — Qualified or disqualified
3. **Demo Completed** — Showed platform, discussed pricing
4. **Proposal Sent** — Sent pricing + next steps email
5. **Negotiation** — Discussing contract, pricing, terms
6. **Closed Won** — Contract signed, payment processed
7. **Closed Lost** — Deal lost (track reason)

**Review weekly:** Which stage has the biggest drop-off? Optimize that stage.
