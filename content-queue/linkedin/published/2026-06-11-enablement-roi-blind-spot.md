## Post Content

You can measure training attendance.

You cannot measure training execution.

Ask any sales leader how they prove their methodology training worked. They'll show you attendance records, certification scores, and NPS ratings from the workshop.

None of those tell you whether a rep actually used the methodology on a live call Tuesday morning.

But the CFO is starting to ask harder questions.

$50K per rep on Sandler training. $30K on Gap Selling workshops. Another $20K on Challenger certification.

What changed, call by call, 90 days later?

Your CRM shows deal stages advancing. It doesn't show whether those deals were properly qualified. 

A rep can move an opportunity to "proposal" without ever running the Pain Funnel. The system doesn't flag that gap.

You're defending one of your largest budget items with satisfaction surveys and quiz scores.

Meanwhile, the methodology you paid for is decaying in real calls, under real pressure, while no one's watching.

Most sales organizations can tell you who showed up to training.

Almost none can tell you who's still using it.
