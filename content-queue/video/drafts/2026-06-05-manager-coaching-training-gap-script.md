TITLE: The Manager Was Never Trained to Coach

HOOK: Only 40% of sales managers were ever trained to coach. The other 60% are improvising.

CONTEXT: You promoted your best rep. She hit 140% three years running. She knew how to find pain, build urgency, close without flinching. So you handed her a team. What you promoted was her ability to sell — not her ability to develop other people. Those are different skills. Nobody told her that.

INSIGHT: When observers sit in on manager one-on-ones, role-play happens in fewer than 15% of sessions. Real-time call review gets replaced by deal status updates. The manager isn't avoiding coaching. She's defaulting to what she was measured on as a rep: pipeline, activity, forecast. Nobody retrained the measurement system when they retrained the title.

CLOSE: The next time you're wondering why coaching isn't happening across your team, ask one question before you assign blame. Who trained your managers to coach?
