# Market Intelligence — One Click Coaching

**Purpose:** Understand competitive landscape, position against alternatives, handle objections with precision, win deals through differentiation.

**Core Principle:** OCC is not a replacement for conversation intelligence. It's the accountability layer that fills the gap between training and execution.

---

## Competitive Landscape

### Tier 1: Conversation Intelligence (Not Direct Competitors)

**Gong, Chorus.ai, Salesloft Conversation Intelligence**
- **What they are:** Manager-focused tools for listening to calls, identifying trends, coaching opportunities
- **What they're not:** Methodology reinforcement systems for reps
- **Buyer:** VP of Sales, Revenue Operations
- **Use case:** Call recording, analytics, manager coaching insights
- **Price:** $100-150/user/month
- **Gap OCC fills:** Gong tells managers what happened. OCC tells reps what to do differently — before the next call.

**Positioning:**
> "Gong is conversation intelligence for managers. One Click Coaching is methodology reinforcement for reps. Different problem, different buyer, different outcome."

### Tier 2: Training Platforms (Not Direct Competitors)

**Allego, Mindtickle, Lessonly**
- **What they are:** Content delivery platforms, onboarding tools, certification systems
- **What they're not:** Call-specific, real-time coaching systems
- **Buyer:** Sales Enablement, L&D
- **Use case:** Training content, playbooks, certification tracking
- **Price:** $50-100/user/month
- **Gap OCC fills:** They teach methodology in a classroom. OCC reinforces it on live calls.

**Positioning:**
> "Training platforms teach methodology once. One Click Coaching reinforces it every call."

### Tier 3: Sales Coaching Tools (Partial Overlap)

**ExecVision, Refract, Jiminny**
- **What they are:** Call recording + analytics + manual coaching workflows
- **What they're not:** Automated, methodology-specific, rep-facing coaching
- **Buyer:** Sales Managers, Sales Ops
- **Use case:** Manager-driven call review, manual coaching feedback
- **Price:** $75-120/user/month
- **Gap OCC fills:** Manual coaching doesn't scale. OCC automates same-day feedback for every rep, every call.

**Positioning:**
> "Manual coaching doesn't scale. One Click Coaching automates methodology reinforcement so managers focus on exceptions, not basics."

### Tier 4: Generic AI Coaching (Direct Competitors)

**Second Nature, Quantified, Rehearsal**
- **What they are:** AI roleplay tools, conversation simulators, generic sales coaching
- **What they're not:** Methodology-specific, call-scoring systems with rubrics
- **Buyer:** Sales Enablement, Frontline Managers
- **Use case:** Roleplay practice, AI conversation partners
- **Price:** $40-80/user/month
- **Gap OCC fills:** Generic AI coaching isn't grounded in your methodology. OCC scores against Sandler (or MEDDIC/SPIN/Challenger) with precision.

**Positioning:**
> "Generic AI coaching is a black box. One Click Coaching scores every call against your methodology — with rubrics, examples, and rep-specific feedback."

## OCC's Unique Position

### The White Space

**What no one else does:**
1. **Deep methodology scoring** — 8-component Sandler rubric (Pain Funnel depth, Budget qualification, Decision Process, etc.)
2. **Non-repetition engine** — Never sends the same coaching twice. Tracks what each rep has received.
3. **Escalation logic** — When a weakness recurs across 3+ calls, escalates to manager for 1:1 intervention.
4. **Manager approval gate** — Human-in-the-loop before coaching fires. No rogue AI feedback.
5. **White-label ready** — Custom logo, colors, company name. Looks like internal tooling.
6. **RAG memory** — Every rep has a persistent memory layer. The system learns over time.

**Core differentiator:**
> "One Click Coaching is the only platform that scores calls against your specific methodology, remembers every rep's coaching history, and escalates persistent weaknesses to managers — all in the same day the call happened."

## Objection Handling Framework

### Objection #1: "We already use Gong."

**Response:**
> "Gong is great for managers to understand what's happening across the team. But it doesn't close the feedback loop with reps. Your reps aren't logging into Gong to self-correct — they're taking calls and repeating the same mistakes. One Click Coaching gives reps same-day feedback on the specific behaviors they need to change, grounded in the methodology you've already trained them on. Gong + OCC is the full stack: intelligence for managers, reinforcement for reps."

**Key point:** OCC complements Gong. Not a replacement.

### Objection #2: "Our managers already coach."

**Response:**
> "Of course they do. But your managers can't listen to every call, score it against the methodology, and give feedback the same day. If you have 10 reps taking 5 calls a day, that's 50 calls a day, 250 a week. Manual coaching doesn't scale. One Click Coaching automates the basics — Pain Funnel depth, budget qualification, decision process — so your managers can focus on high-value coaching: strategic deals, career development, edge cases."

**Key point:** OCC extends manager capacity. Doesn't replace it.

### Objection #3: "We're not sure our team will use it."

**Response:**
> "That's the right concern. Most AI tools fail because reps don't trust the feedback. That's why we built OCC with a manager approval gate. Before any coaching fires, the manager reviews it. If it's off, they kill it. If it's spot on, they approve it. Reps get feedback they trust because their manager vouched for it. Over time, as the system learns, managers approve faster. Eventually, it's just running."

**Key point:** Trust is built through the manager approval layer.

### Objection #4: "How is this different from roleplay tools?"

**Response:**
> "Roleplay tools are practice. One Click Coaching is performance feedback. Roleplay happens before the call. OCC scores what actually happened on the call — with the real prospect, real objections, real pressure. It's the difference between a practice swing and film review after the at-bat."

**Key point:** OCC operates on real call data, not simulations.

### Objection #5: "What if we don't use Sandler?"

**Response:**
> "Right now, Sandler is the only fully built methodology. MEDDIC, SPIN, and Challenger are on the roadmap, but not live yet. If your team uses Sandler, OCC is the perfect fit today. If you use something else, we can talk about timeline — but I won't sell you something that's not ready."

**Key point:** Honesty builds trust. Don't oversell.

### Objection #6: "What does it cost?"

**Response:**
> "Pricing depends on team size. Starter tier covers up to 10 reps at $249/month. Growth tier goes to 25 reps at $489/month. We also have per-rep pricing at $50/rep/month depending on your structure. Worth a 20-minute call to see if it fits your use case before we talk numbers in detail."

**Key point:** Anchor pricing, but defer to discovery call for precision.

### Objection #7: "Does it integrate with [our CRM/tools]?"

**Response:**
> "We're integrated with HubSpot and Fathom right now. Aircall and Fireflies are on the roadmap. If you're using something else, let's talk about your stack on a quick call — I'll tell you what's built and what's not."

**Key point:** Name what's live. Don't promise what's not built.

### Objection #8: "We tried AI coaching before and it didn't work."

**Response:**
> "Most AI coaching tools are black boxes. Reps get feedback but don't know why. When they can't see the rubric or the methodology behind it, they ignore it. One Click Coaching is transparent: every score shows the rubric, shows examples of what good looks like, and ties back to the methodology your team already knows. Transparency isn't a feature — it's why reps actually use it."

**Key point:** Transparency = trust = adoption.

## When OCC Wins

### Buying Signals (Green Lights)

1. **"We invested in [Sandler/MEDDIC/Challenger] training and ROI faded fast."**
   - They've already spent money on methodology. They're primed for reinforcement.

2. **"Our top reps are great, but the middle 60% is inconsistent."**
   - Methodology drift. Perfect use case.

3. **"Our managers don't have time to listen to every call."**
   - Capacity problem. OCC extends manager leverage.

4. **"We use Gong but reps aren't changing behavior."**
   - Gong shows the problem. OCC fixes it.

5. **"Our onboarding is strong but new reps backslide after 90 days."**
   - Classic reinforcement gap. OCC closes it.

6. **"We have a defined methodology but reps revert to pitching under pressure."**
   - Pressure reveals habit. OCC builds habit.

### Red Flags (Disqualify Fast)

1. **"We don't have a defined sales methodology."**
   - OCC reinforces methodology. If they don't have one, OCC can't help.

2. **"We're a startup with 2 reps."**
   - Too small. OCC is for teams of 5+.

3. **"We want AI to replace our managers."**
   - Wrong expectation. OCC extends managers, doesn't replace them.

4. **"We need this to work with [obscure CRM/tool]."**
   - If integration doesn't exist, disqualify or defer.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Firmographic
- **Company size:** 50-500 employees
- **Sales team size:** 5-25 reps
- **Revenue:** $5M-$50M ARR
- **Industry:** B2B SaaS, financial services, professional services, manufacturing (complex sales)
- **Sales cycle:** 30-120 days (not transactional)

### Psychographic
- **Pain:** Methodology training ROI is eroding
- **Sophistication:** Already trained team on Sandler/MEDDIC/SPIN/Challenger
- **Budget:** Spent $10-20k+ per rep on training
- **Authority:** VP of Sales, CRO, Sales Enablement Director
- **Tooling:** Using CRM (HubSpot/Salesforce), maybe Gong/Chorus, maybe not
- **Trigger:** Recent training investment, new methodology rollout, inconsistent rep performance

### Anti-ICP (Disqualify)
- Transactional sales (< 15 min calls, high volume)
- No defined methodology
- Sales team < 5 reps
- Self-serve SaaS (no human sales process)
- Unwilling to have manager involved (wants fully automated AI)

## Discovery Call Qualification Script

**Use this to qualify fast:**

**Question 1:** "What sales methodology is your team trained on?"
- ✓ If Sandler → Perfect fit, proceed.
- ✗ If none → "OCC reinforces methodology. Without one, it won't work. Let's revisit when you've installed a methodology."

**Question 2:** "What's the pain you're trying to solve?"
- ✓ If "reps revert to old habits" or "training ROI fades" → Core pain, proceed.
- ✗ If "we need call recording" → Wrong tool, recommend Gong.

**Question 3:** "How many reps do you have?"
- ✓ If 5-25 → ICP range, proceed.
- ✗ If < 5 → "Let's revisit when you hit 5+ reps."

**Question 4:** "What tools are you using today for coaching?"
- ✓ If Gong/Chorus → "OCC complements that. Gong for managers, OCC for reps."
- ✓ If manual coaching → "OCC automates the basics so managers focus on high-value coaching."
- ✗ If fully automated AI → "OCC has a manager approval gate. If you want zero human involvement, this isn't the right fit."

**Question 5:** "What does success look like in 6 months?"
- ✓ If "reps consistently execute methodology" → Perfect outcome expectation.
- ✗ If "AI replaces our managers" → Wrong expectation, disqualify.
