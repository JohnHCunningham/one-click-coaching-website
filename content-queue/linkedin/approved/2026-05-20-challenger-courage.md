Challenger training doesn't fail in the classroom. It fails in the follow-up email.

The rep walks out of training ready to teach, tailor, and take control. For a few weeks, they bring commercial insight into every conversation. They reframe the customer's thinking instead of just answering questions. They create the constructive tension that Challenger depends on.

Then the pipeline tightens.

And suddenly "teach for differentiation" becomes "position the features." "Tailor for resonance" becomes "send the same deck." The commercial teaching that should set them apart gets replaced by the safest thing that might close a deal.

The trainers who teach Challenger know this happens. They deliver the methodology, then watch it get smoothed into standard solution selling the moment pressure returns.

Challenger is the methodology that demands the most courage from a rep. And courage is the first thing pipeline pressure kills.
