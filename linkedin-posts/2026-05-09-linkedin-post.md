## Post Content

87% of sales training content is forgotten within 30 days.

Here's what most organizations miss: They train for knowledge transfer, not behavior change.

The brain doesn't learn through information dumps. It learns through repetition, application, and reinforcement. Yet we design training like we're filling empty vessels instead of rewiring neural pathways.

Consider this: A rep can perfectly recite your value proposition in training but still default to feature-dumping under pressure. Why? Because knowing isn't doing, and doing isn't sustained doing.

The companies that crack this code don't just train differently—they reinforce systematically:
→ Micro-learning over marathon sessions
→ Practice over presentation
→ Coaching over content delivery

You can't train your way to lasting behavior change. But you can reinforce your way there.

What's your experience with training retention in your organization?
