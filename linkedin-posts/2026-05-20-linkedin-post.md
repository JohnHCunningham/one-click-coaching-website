## Post Content

87% of sales training content is forgotten within 30 days.

Yet most organizations measure training success by attendance rates, satisfaction scores, and immediate knowledge retention.

Here's what neuroscience tells us about behavior change:

The forgetting curve is steep, swift, and unforgiving. Without reinforcement, new behaviors decay exponentially—not gradually.

Memory consolidation requires repetition. Skill application requires practice. Behavior change requires reinforcement.

But here's the paradox: We front-load the learning and back-load the application. We concentrate the content and scatter the support.

Top-performing sales organizations flip this formula. They deliver less content more often. They embed reinforcement in workflow. They make practice inevitable, not optional.

The brain doesn't learn through information dumps. It learns through spaced retrieval, contextual application, and systematic reinforcement.

Stop measuring training by what people know. Start measuring by what people do differently.

What reinforcement mechanisms are built into your sales training strategy?
