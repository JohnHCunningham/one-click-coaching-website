# OCC Content Strategy — LinkedIn System

## The Five Pillars

| Pillar | Purpose | Attracts |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Neuroscience / Data | Credibility. You're the expert. | "What's the science behind this?" |
| Story + Insight | Emotion. They see themselves. | "This is exactly my team." |
| Contrarian Take | Authority. You challenge the room. | "I've never thought of it that way." |
| Thought Leadership | Vision. You see what's coming. | "Where is this going?" |
| Social Proof / Outcome | Evidence. You've done it. | "Can you show me the numbers?" |

## Weekly Rotation (3x/week)

| Monday | Wednesday | Friday |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Neuroscience or Thought Leadership | Story + Insight | Contrarian or Social Proof |

Social proof weaves into whichever post it fits — it doesn't need its own slot every week.

## Round Structure

- **Round 1** — Names the problem. Establishes the gap. Creates the question.
- **Round 2** — Shows the mechanism. Explains why the problem persists.
- **Round 3** — Shows the solution in action. Names OCC explicitly.

Never pitch the product in Round 1. Never.

## Hook Principles

Curiosity through restraint. Four hook styles that work:

1. **The Number** — "72 hours." / "37 calls. One pattern."
2. **The Incomplete Statement** — "He'd been through every program they offered."
3. **The Provocation** — "More coaching isn't the problem."
4. **The Scene** — Drop into a specific moment. No context. Just presence.

## Post Architecture

```
Hook (1–2 lines — creates the tension)
↓
Setup (2–3 lines — the situation most readers recognize)
↓
Pivot (1 line — the turn. Often starts with "But." or "Except.")
↓
Insight (2–3 lines — the truth they hadn't named)
↓
Landing line (1 line — the aphorism. Standalone. Memorable.)
↓
CTA (comment-only, low friction, curiosity-extending)
```

## CTA System

Never: links, DMs, "book a call", "check out my product."

Always: "Drop a comment if you want to see what that looks like."
Or: "Curious if others have seen the same thing — drop a comment."
Or: "Happy to walk you through it if you want to see it live."

## Comment-to-Call Conversion

The sequence: Post creates curiosity → comment asks question → public reply deepens
the question → private conversation opens → discovery call booked.

**The rule:** Answer every comment publicly in a way that makes the next question inevitable.
Never answer fully. Leave the mechanism unexplained. End with a low-friction live invite.

**Response template structure:**
1. Affirm the question (1 line — shows you read it)
2. Partial answer — enough to be useful, not enough to be complete (2–3 lines)
3. The live invite — "Happy to show you what that looks like in practice."

**Question → Response mapping:**

| Question | Response angle |
|----------|---------------|
| "What does the scoring output look like?" | Name the 8 components, tease the non-repetition engine, invite them to see it live |
| "How is this different from Gong?" | Gong is conversation intelligence for managers. OCC is coaching automation for reps. Different problem, different buyer. |
| "What does it cost?" | Depends on team size. Starter tier covers teams up to 10 reps. Worth a 20-minute conversation to see if it fits. |
| "Does it work with [tool]?" | Name what's built (HubSpot, Fathom, Aircall, Fireflies). Invite them to confirm their stack on a quick call. |
| "Is it only Sandler?" | Sandler is the fully built methodology. Others are on the roadmap. If your team uses Sandler, it's the right fit now. |

## Rhetorical Devices Requirement (All Content)

**Every piece of content must include rhetorical devices:**
- **LinkedIn posts:** 3-5 devices
- **Blog posts:** 4-6 devices (enforced by blog.md)
- **Cold emails:** 2-4 devices (enforced by outreach.md)
- **Landing pages:** 4-6 devices
- **Ads:** 2-3 devices (shorter format)

**The OCC Rhetorical Device Toolkit:**

### 1. Antithesis (Contrasting Ideas)
Creates clarity through opposition. Most versatile device.

**Examples:**
- "Training teaches knowledge. Coaching builds execution."
- "Hope is not a strategy. Habit is."
- "Your CRM tracks activity. OCC changes behavior."

**Use in:** Headlines, key insights, closing lines

### 2. Tricolon (Rule of Three)
Creates rhythm and memorability.

**Examples:**
- "Measure what matters. Reinforce before drift. Coach while the moment is alive."
- "Not because they didn't care. Not because training was bad. Because reinforcement never came."
- "Same methodology. Same reps. Different results."

**Use in:** Lists, problem statements, CTAs

### 3. Anaphora (Repetition at Beginning)
Builds momentum and emphasis.

**Examples:**
- "Your reps know the methodology. Your reps believe in it. Your reps just don't execute it."
- "Not because of skill. Not because of effort. Because of system."

**Use in:** Building emotional weight, emphasizing patterns

### 4. Metaphor (Direct Comparison)
Makes abstract concrete. No "like" or "as."

**Examples:**
- "Your CRM is a graveyard of good intentions."
- "Pressure is the X-ray of sales training."
- "Methodology is the skeleton. Habit is the muscle."

**Use in:** Problem statements, making concepts vivid

### 5. Epistrophe (Repetition at End)
Anchors message through repeated endings.

**Examples:**
- "Performance doesn't change in hindsight. It changes in execution. It changes when it matters."
- "You can measure it. You can track it. You can improve it."

**Use in:** Conclusions, CTAs, vision statements

### 6. Anadiplosis (Link Sentences)
End of one sentence becomes beginning of next.

**Examples:**
- "Training creates knowledge. Knowledge without reinforcement becomes forgotten theory."
- "Behavior becomes habit. Habit becomes performance."

**Use in:** Building logical flow, creating inevitability

**How to Use:**
1. Write first draft (no devices)
2. Identify 3-5 key sentences to enhance
3. Apply devices to those sentences
4. Read aloud - if it sounds forced, remove it
5. Devices should feel natural, not decorative

## Landing Page & Ad Copy Frameworks

### Landing Page Copy Structure

**Every OCC landing page follows this sequence:**

1. **Hero Section** (Above the fold)
   - Headline with antithesis or metaphor
   - Subheadline with problem statement
   - CTA (primary action)
   - Social proof (trust badge, customer logo, stat)

2. **Problem Section**
   - Agitate the pain (use anaphora for emphasis)
   - Quantify the cost (lost revenue, wasted time, eroded ROI)
   - Include specific example or story

3. **Solution Section** (How OCC Works)
   - 3-step process (tricolon)
   - Visual flow diagram
   - Emphasize simplicity and speed

4. **Features/Benefits**
   - Feature = what it is
   - Benefit = what it does for them
   - Format: "Feature → Benefit" (use arrow for causality)

5. **Social Proof**
   - Customer testimonials (specific results)
   - Case studies (before/after with numbers)
   - Logos of users (if available)

6. **Objection Handling** (FAQ)
   - Address 3-5 common objections
   - Keep answers 2-3 sentences
   - Be direct, not defensive

7. **CTA Section**
   - Restate transformation
   - Clear next step
   - Remove friction (free trial, demo, pilot)

### The 3 Core Copywriting Frameworks

#### Framework 1: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

**Use for:** Landing pages, email sequences, long-form sales copy

**Structure:**
1. **Problem:** Name it better than they can
2. **Agitate:** Show the cost of inaction (loss aversion)
3. **Solution:** OCC as inevitable answer

**Example:**
```
Problem: "Your reps completed Sandler training 90 days ago. Today, they're back to pitching features."

Agitate: "That's $15k per rep in training ROI evaporating. Your managers don't have time to listen to every call. Your CRM tracks activity, not methodology execution. The gap between training and performance is widening."

Solution: "One Click Coaching scores every call against Sandler, fires same-day feedback to reps, and escalates recurring weaknesses to managers. Training finally sticks."
```

**Rhetorical devices to include:**
- Antithesis in problem statement
- Anaphora in agitation ("Your managers... Your CRM... Your training...")
- Tricolon in solution

---

#### Framework 2: AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

**Use for:** Ads, short landing pages, email sequences

**Structure:**
1. **Attention:** Hook with number, provocation, or question
2. **Interest:** Show them you understand their world
3. **Desire:** Paint the after-state (transformation)
4. **Action:** Clear CTA with low friction

**Example:**
```
Attention: "87% of sales training is forgotten in 30 days."

Interest: "You invested $200k in Sandler. Your reps loved it. Within 90 days, they're back to pitching features. Not because they forgot. Because reinforcement never came."

Desire: "Imagine: Every rep executes the methodology under pressure. Managers focus on strategy, not basics. Your training ROI is measurable and sustained."

Action: "See what same-day coaching looks like. Book 20-minute demo."
```

**Rhetorical devices to include:**
- Number hook for attention
- Metaphor for interest ("Training has a half-life")
- Anaphora for desire ("Every rep... Every call... Every day...")

---

#### Framework 3: BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

**Use for:** Case studies, testimonials, transformation stories

**Structure:**
1. **Before:** The pain state (specific, quantified)
2. **After:** The transformed state (specific, quantified)
3. **Bridge:** How OCC got them there

**Example:**
```
Before: "{{Company}} trained 15 reps on Sandler. Within 60 days, only 3 were executing Pain Funnel consistently. Managers spent 15 hours/week manually coaching. Close rate: 18%."

After: "Today, 12 of 15 reps execute Pain Funnel on every call. Manager coaching time: 5 hours/week (focused on strategic deals). Close rate: 27%."

Bridge: "One Click Coaching scored every call, gave same-day feedback, and escalated persistent weaknesses. The training they'd already paid for finally stuck."
```

**Rhetorical devices to include:**
- Antithesis (Before vs After)
- Tricolon in bridge ("Scored... Gave... Escalated...")
- Specific numbers (no vague claims)

### Ad Copy Best Practices

- Clear CTA

**Example:**

- Match search intent exactly
- Include keyword

- Visual carries weight
- Copy emphasizes transformation
- One powerful device

**Example:**
```

(Antithesis + Tricolon)
```

### CTA Optimization Framework

**Weak CTAs:**
- ❌ "Learn More" (vague)
- ❌ "Get Started" (commitment unclear)
- ❌ "Sign Up" (too much friction)

**Strong CTAs:**
- ✅ "See What Reps Receive" (specific)
- ✅ "Book 20-Minute Demo" (time-bound)
- ✅ "Start 30-Day Pilot" (low-risk)

**CTA Formula:**
```
[Action Verb] + [Specific Outcome] + [Low Friction Element]
```

**Examples:**
- "See what same-day coaching looks like" (See + specific + no commitment)
- "Book a 20-minute demo, no pitch" (Book + time-bound + explicit no-pressure)
- "Start 30-day pilot with 5 reps" (Start + defined scope + small scale)

**Placement:**
- Hero section (primary CTA)
- After problem agitation (secondary CTA)
- After social proof (tertiary CTA)
- Final section (primary CTA repeated)

## Human Voice Quality Control (Target: 9.5/10)

**Goal:** Content should read like it was written by an experienced sales leader, not AI.

### The Human Voice Checklist

**✅ DO (Sounds Human):**

1. **Use Contractions Naturally**
   - "You've invested" not "You have invested"
   - "They'll execute" not "They will execute"
   - "It's not" not "It is not"
   - Natural speech rhythm over formal grammar

2. **Vary Sentence Length Dramatically**
   - Short punches: "Training fades."
   - Medium flow: "Your reps completed Sandler 90 days ago."
   - Long build: "Not because they didn't care, not because the training was wrong, but because the system that reinforced it never existed."
   - Human writing has rhythm, not uniformity

3. **Include Imperfect Phrasing**
   - Start sentences with "And" or "But" occasionally
   - Use fragments for emphasis: "Not once. Not ever."
   - End sentences with prepositions if natural: "What are you waiting for?"
   - Real humans break grammar rules for effect

4. **Use Specific, Concrete Details**
   - "Your manager spent 12 hours last week listening to calls"
   - Not: "Managers spend significant time reviewing calls"
   - Numbers, names, timestamps = human
   - Vague generalities = AI

5. **Show Personality Through Word Choice**
   - "Your CRM is a graveyard" (personality)
   - Not: "Your CRM contains unused data" (sterile)
   - Pick vivid verbs: "evaporates" not "decreases"
   - Strong nouns: "gap" not "difference"

6. **Write Like You're Talking to One Person**
   - "You" not "companies" or "sales leaders"
   - Second person, direct address
   - Conversational, not academic

7. **Include Natural Asides**
   - Parenthetical thoughts: "Your reps (all 15 of them) know the methodology."
   - Em dashes for emphasis: "Training sticks — until pressure shows up."
   - Feels like a conversation, not a lecture

8. **Use Questions That Sound Natural**
   - "What happened between Tuesday and Monday morning?"
   - Not: "Have you considered the factors contributing to performance decline?"
   - Ask questions you'd actually ask a friend

---

### ❌ AVOID (AI Tells)

**These phrases scream "AI-generated" — never use them:**

1. **Overused AI Clichés:**
   - ❌ "In today's fast-paced world"
   - ❌ "In conclusion"
   - ❌ "It's important to note that"
   - ❌ "At the end of the day"
   - ❌ "Let's dive in" / "Let's dive deep"
   - ❌ "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary"
   - ❌ "Unlock the power of"
   - ❌ "Seamlessly integrate"
   - ❌ "Cutting-edge" / "State-of-the-art"
   - ❌ "Leverage" (unless genuinely appropriate)

2. **Generic Transitions:**
   - ❌ "Furthermore" / "Moreover" / "Additionally"
   - ✅ Use: "And" / "But" / "So" / or just start new paragraph

3. **Hedging Language:**
   - ❌ "It's worth noting that"
   - ❌ "One might argue"
   - ❌ "Generally speaking"
   - ✅ Be direct: "Your reps revert to pitching."

4. **Listicle Formatting (Overused by AI):**
   - ❌ "Here are 5 key strategies:"
   - ❌ "3 reasons why X matters:"
   - ✅ Integrate points into narrative flow instead

5. **Overly Enthusiastic Language:**
   - ❌ Exclamation marks (except in rare emphasis)
   - ❌ "Amazing!" / "Incredible!" / "Fantastic!"
   - ✅ Calm confidence over hype

6. **Perfect Grammar (Ironically):**
   - ❌ Every sentence perfectly structured
   - ❌ No fragments, no rule-breaking
   - ✅ Humans break rules. Do it intentionally.

### The Human Voice Formula

**Opening 3 Sentences = Make or Break**

If first 3 sentences sound AI, reader bounces. Humans decide in 10 seconds.

**Bad (AI-sounding) Opening:**
> "In today's competitive sales environment, organizations are increasingly recognizing the importance of effective sales training. However, many companies struggle to see lasting results from their training investments. This article explores why sales training often fails to deliver ROI."

**Good (Human) Opening:**
> "Your reps completed Sandler training 90 days ago. Today, they're pitching features. That $15k per rep? Gone."

**What makes it human:**
- Second person (you)
- Short, punchy sentences
- Specific number ($15k)
- Blunt conclusion (Gone.)
- Sounds like a conversation opener

---

### The "Read Aloud" Test (Mandatory)

**Before publishing ANY content:**

1. Read it out loud
2. If you stumble, rewrite that sentence
3. If it sounds like a textbook, rewrite it
4. If you wouldn't say it to a colleague, rewrite it

**Human writing flows when spoken. AI writing sounds stilted.**

---

### Personality Markers (Add These)

1. **Occasional Sarcasm (Light Touch)**
   - "Your CRM tracks every click. Wonderful. Now where's the behavior change?"
   - Use sparingly, only where appropriate

2. **Pattern Recognition Voice**
   - "I've seen this 50 times."
   - "Same story. Different company."
   - Shows experience, not theory

3. **Direct Challenges**
   - "Stop lying to yourself."
   - "You know this isn't working."
   - Humans confront. AI softens.

4. **Admissions and Honesty**
   - "This is hard to implement."
   - "Most teams fail at this."
   - AI overpromises. Humans acknowledge difficulty.

### The Humanness Score Rubric

**9.5/10 = Human Expert Writing**

Check every piece against this:

- [ ] Uses contractions naturally (5+ times per 500 words)
- [ ] Varies sentence length (some < 5 words, some > 25 words)
- [ ] Includes at least 1 sentence fragment for emphasis
- [ ] Starts 1-2 sentences with "And" or "But"
- [ ] Uses specific numbers, not ranges (12 hours, not "10-15 hours")
- [ ] Includes vivid verbs (evaporate, hemorrhage, revert)
- [ ] Second person throughout (you, your)
- [ ] Contains zero AI cliché phrases
- [ ] Passes read-aloud test (flows when spoken)
- [ ] Sounds like a senior sales leader talking to a peer

**If 9+ boxes checked:** 9.5/10 human ✅
**If 7-8 boxes:** 8/10 (revise problem areas)
**If < 7 boxes:** AI-detected (rewrite from scratch)

---

### Final Polish: The Personality Pass

**After writing, do one final edit for personality:**

1. Find 3 places to make it more direct (cut hedge words)
2. Find 2 places to add concrete details (numbers, names, timestamps)
3. Find 1 place to show experience ("I've seen this pattern...")
4. Remove any phrase that sounds "corporate"
5. Read opening 3 sentences aloud - would you actually say this?

**If opening doesn't grab you, it won't grab them.**

### Examples: AI Voice vs Human Voice

**Topic: Sales training ROI**

**AI Voice (6/10):**
> "Organizations often invest significant resources in sales training programs, expecting to see substantial improvements in performance. However, research indicates that the effectiveness of such training tends to diminish over time. This phenomenon can be attributed to various factors, including lack of reinforcement and inadequate follow-up mechanisms."

**Human Voice (9.5/10):**
> "You spent $200k on Sandler training. Your reps loved it. Ninety days later, they're back to pitching features. Not because they forgot. Because nothing reinforced it after Tuesday's workshop."

**What changed:**
- Second person (you)
- Specific number ($200k, 90 days)
- Contractions (they're, didn't)
- Fragment for emphasis (Not because...)
- Blunt, conversational tone
- Cause clear and direct

---

**Topic: Manual coaching doesn't scale**

**AI Voice (5/10):**
> "As sales teams grow in size, the traditional approach of manual coaching becomes increasingly challenging to maintain. Sales managers often find themselves overwhelmed by the volume of calls that require review and feedback. This scalability issue presents a significant challenge for growing organizations."

**Human Voice (9.5/10):**
> "Your managers can't listen to 250 calls a week. The math doesn't work. If you have 10 reps taking 5 calls a day, that's 50 calls daily. Even at 30 minutes per call review, that's 25 hours. Per day. Manual coaching doesn't scale. It breaks."

**What changed:**
- Direct address (your managers)
- Specific math (10 reps, 5 calls, 25 hours)
- Short sentences (It breaks.)
- Conversational explanation (The math doesn't work.)
- No hedging (doesn't scale vs. "becomes challenging")

### Integration with Rhetorical Devices

**Devices + Human voice = Peak persuasion**

Good:
- Antithesis: "Training teaches. OCC reinforces."

Better (more human):
- "Training teaches knowledge. Execution? That's a different problem."

**The difference:**
- Contraction (That's)
- Question format (natural speech)
- Conversational flow

**All rhetorical devices should sound natural when spoken aloud.**

---

### Red Flags: Rewrite Immediately If You See These

1. ❌ Three paragraphs start the same way
2. ❌ No contractions in entire piece
3. ❌ Every sentence is 15-20 words (uniform length)
4. ❌ Uses "In conclusion" or "In summary"
5. ❌ Contains "game-changer" or "unlock"
6. ❌ Sounds like a college essay, not a conversation
7. ❌ You wouldn't say it out loud to a colleague

**If you see 2+ red flags: Start over. Don't edit. Rewrite.**

---

### The Ultimate Test: The 24-Hour Read

**For critical content (landing pages, key emails, major posts):**

1. Write it
2. Walk away for 24 hours
3. Read it fresh the next day
4. Ask: "Did a real person write this, or did AI?"

**If you hesitate for even a second: Revise.**

Human voice isn't about perfection. It's about authenticity, rhythm, and personality.

**Target: 9.5/10. Settle for nothing less.**
