# Partner Strategy — One Click Coaching

**Purpose:** Build distribution channel through Sandler Training franchisees who have direct relationships with qualified buyers.

**Principle:** Sandler franchisees win when their clients get ROI. OCC delivers measurable ROI. Alignment is natural.

---

## The Partner Value Proposition

### Why Sandler Franchisees Should Care

**The Problem They Face:**
Sandler franchisees install methodology training. Clients love the initial results. Then, 90 days later, performance plateaus. Clients question the ROI. Renewals become objection-handling exercises instead of expansions.

**The Gap:**
Franchisees teach the methodology. They don't have a system to reinforce it daily. Manual coaching doesn't scale. Clients need proof that training is sticking. Franchisees can't provide it.

**The Solution (OCC):**
One Click Coaching is the accountability layer that proves methodology is being executed. Franchisees can now say:
> "We install Sandler. One Click Coaching makes sure your reps execute it — every call, every day. Here's the data to prove it."

**The Outcome for Franchisees:**
- **Higher renewal rates** — Clients see measurable ROI (methodology execution scores, behavior change over time)
- **Expansion revenue** — "You've trained 10 reps. Let's add OCC for all 25."
- **Differentiation** — No other Sandler franchisee offers AI-powered reinforcement
- **Stickiness** — OCC locks in the methodology. Harder for clients to switch to MEDDIC or SPIN if OCC is already scoring Sandler.

**Option 1: Referral Fee (One-Time)**
- Franchisee refers client → OCC closes deal → Franchisee gets 20% of first year contract value
- Example: $489/month Growth plan × 12 months = $5,868 contract → $1,173 referral fee

**Option 2: Revenue Share (Recurring)**
- Franchisee refers client → OCC closes deal → Franchisee gets 10-15% of MRR as long as client is active
- Example: $489/month Growth plan → $49-73/month to franchisee (ongoing)

**Option 3: White-Label Reseller**
- Franchisee sells OCC directly to their clients under their brand
- Franchisee sets price (suggested $60-80/rep/month)
- OCC charges franchisee wholesale ($35-40/rep/month)
- Franchisee keeps margin ($20-40/rep/month per client)

**Recommendation:** Start with Option 1 (referral fee) for simplicity. Scale to Option 3 (white-label) once 5+ franchisees are active.

## Partner Discovery Call Structure (30 Minutes)

**Goal:** Qualify franchisee as partner, show value prop, align on next steps.

---

### Phase 1: Rapport + Agenda (Minutes 0-2)

**Opening:**
> "Thanks for making time. Here's how I'd like to use the next 30 minutes: I'll ask you a few questions about your client base, show you what OCC does, and if it makes sense, we'll talk about how to partner. Sound good?"

---

### Phase 2: Partner Qualification (Minutes 2-10)

**The 4 Partner Qualification Questions:**

#### Q1: "How many active clients do you have right now?"

**Listen for:**
- ✓ 10-30 active clients → Good volume for partnership
- ✗ < 10 clients → Too small, revisit when they scale
- ✗ 50+ clients → Likely already have internal solutions or other partnerships

#### Q2: "What's the typical profile of your clients? Team size, industry, sales process?"

**Listen for:**
- ✓ B2B companies, 5-25 rep teams, complex sales → Perfect OCC ICP
- ✗ Transactional sales, < 5 reps, B2C → Not OCC's target market

#### Q3: "What's the biggest objection you hear from clients 6-12 months after training?"

**Listen for:**
- ✓ "We don't see sustained ROI" → OCC solves this
- ✓ "Reps revert to old habits" → OCC solves this
- ✓ "We need proof it's working" → OCC provides this

#### Q4: "Have you explored technology partnerships before? What worked or didn't?"

**Listen for:**
- ✓ Open to partnerships, understands value → Move forward
- ✗ Skeptical of technology, prefers manual-only approach → Unlikely partner

**If 3+ green lights, proceed. If not, nurture or disqualify.**

---

### Phase 3: OCC Value Prop (Minutes 10-20)

**Show, don't tell.**

**Screen share: OCC demo**

**Step 1: Show Call Score (3 min)**
> "Here's how it works. One of your clients' reps takes a call. OCC scores it against the 8 Sandler components — Pain Funnel, Budget, Decision, all of it. This rep got a 6.2 out of 10. Here's why."

**Point out:**
- Specific components scored (Pain Funnel depth: 4/10, Budget qualification: 8/10, etc.)
- Rubrics show what good looks like
- Timestamps show where rep succeeded or missed

**Tie to their pain:**
> "This is the proof your clients are asking for. They can see exactly where reps are executing Sandler and where they're not."

**Step 2: Show Coaching Output (2 min)**
> "Based on this score, here's the coaching that fires to the rep — same day the call happened."

**Point out:**
- Coaching is Sandler-specific (not generic AI feedback)
- Manager approves before it sends
- Non-repetition engine tracks what rep has been coached on

**Tie to their value:**
> "This extends your engagement. You teach Sandler. OCC reinforces it daily. Your clients get ongoing value, not a one-time workshop."

**Step 3: White-Label Preview (2 min)**
> "One more thing — this can be white-labeled. Your logo, your colors, your company name. Your clients never know OCC exists. It looks like part of your Sandler package."

**Why this matters:** Franchisee owns the client relationship. OCC stays invisible.

---

### Phase 4: Partnership Options (Minutes 20-28)

**Present 3 options:**

**Option 1: Referral Partner**
> "You refer clients to OCC. We close the deal. You get a 20% referral fee on the first year contract. Easiest to start, no operational lift on your side."

**Option 2: Co-Sell Partner**
> "You introduce OCC in your client engagements. We demo together. You get ongoing revenue share (10-15% of MRR). More involved, but recurring revenue."

**Option 3: White-Label Reseller**
> "You sell OCC under your brand. Set your own pricing. We charge you wholesale. You keep the margin. Fully integrated into your offering."

**Ask:**
> "Which model makes the most sense for you?"

**Listen for:**
- Most will start with Option 1 (low risk, easy)
- Advanced partners go straight to Option 3 (full control)

---

### Phase 5: Next Steps (Minutes 28-30)

**If interested:**
> "Here's what I'd suggest: Let's run a pilot with 1-2 of your clients. You introduce OCC, we handle the demo and onboarding, you see the results. If it works, we formalize the partnership. Does that work?"

**Ask for 2 pilot clients:**
- Recent Sandler training (within 6 months)
- 5-15 reps (manageable pilot size)
- Pain visible (methodology drift, manual coaching doesn't scale)

**Next step:**
> "Can you intro me to those 2 clients by end of week? I'll take it from there."

**Book follow-up:**
> "Let's sync in 2 weeks after the pilots kick off. Does {{date}} work?"

## Partner Objection Handling

### Objection: "My clients won't pay for another tool."

**Response:**
> "I get it. But here's the thing: they've already paid for Sandler training. If that training isn't sticking, they've wasted money. OCC makes sure the training sticks. It's not another tool — it's ROI protection for the investment they've already made. Frame it as: 'You've spent $15k/rep on training. Spend $50/rep/month to make sure it works.'"

### Objection: "I don't want to introduce a tool I don't control."

**Response:**
> "That's exactly why we offer white-label. Your clients see your brand, not OCC. You control the relationship. We're invisible. You own the client experience."

### Objection: "What if my clients don't like it?"

**Response:**
> "That's why we pilot first. 1-2 clients, 30 days. If they don't like it, we kill it. No long-term commitment. But every pilot we've run so far has converted. Clients see value immediately."

### Objection: "I'm worried about taking margin from my existing engagements."

**Response:**
> "OCC isn't a replacement for your training. It's an add-on. Your clients still pay you for Sandler training. OCC is the reinforcement layer that makes your training more valuable. You're not losing margin — you're adding a new revenue stream."

## Integration with Direct Sales

**When to prioritize partners over direct sales:**
- Warm intro from Sandler franchisee > cold outreach
- Franchisee-referred client closes 2x faster than direct sale
- Franchisee can co-sell, reducing OCC's sales effort

**When to prioritize direct sales:**
- Client not connected to Sandler franchisee
- Client using MEDDIC/SPIN/Challenger (no franchisee relationship)
- Enterprise deal (50+ reps) where partner can't support

**Rule:** If franchisee intro exists, let partner lead. If not, OCC closes direct.
