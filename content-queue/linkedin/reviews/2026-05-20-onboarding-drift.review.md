## Final Revised Post

He passed the certification.

Two weeks of Sandler training. Role plays every day. The whole team graduated together.

Month one on the floor: sharp.

Upfront contracts. Clean pain questions. Next steps with teeth.

Month two, the pressure arrived.

A deal slipped. Pipeline reviews got tight. A manager asked about the forecast.

And the methodology began to disappear — not dramatically, just gradually.

Pain questions got shallower.
Demos started ten minutes too early.
Next steps sounded professional, but the buyer had not actually committed.

The CRM still looked clean. The stage moved.

But the behavior had already left the building.

Workshops install the methodology. The floor runs a different operating system. And year after year, onboarding class after onboarding class, the same drift repeats — not because the training was weak, but because there was no reinforcement loop waiting on the other side.

Training is not the finish line. It is the starting line. Without reinforcement, even the best methodology becomes a memory within months.

What did you see the last time a new hire went through training — did the behaviors hold?
