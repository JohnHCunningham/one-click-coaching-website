## BLOG — The Re-Training Tax

Write a 1,200-word blog post for One Click Coaching.

**Topic:** Companies buy the same training twice and do not realize it. The re-training tax — when methodology decay forces a second training investment because the first one was never reinforced.

**Hook:** You spent $50,000 on sales training last year. This year, the budget has another line item with a different vendor name — but it is the same training. Same concepts. Same frameworks. Different branding. You just paid for it twice.

**Voice:** Calm, authoritative, no hype. Write like a veteran sales leader who has watched this cycle repeat across companies for decades. You are not selling — you are naming a financial pattern they have never quantified.

**Structure:**
1. Open with a scene: a VP Sales reviewing next year's training budget. A new vendor. New materials. Same underlying methodology. The realization that they are buying the same thing again.
2. Name the re-training tax: companies spend on methodology training every 18-24 months not because the training was bad, but because it was never reinforced. The skills decayed. The new training is just a reset.
3. Show the math: $2,500-3,000 per rep × 15 reps = $37,500-45,000. Every 18-24 months. Over 5 years, that is close to six figures — for the same methodology, taught again.
4. Explain why this happens: training teaches knowledge. Reinforcement builds execution. Without reinforcement, execution decays. Decay looks like a training failure. The fix looks like more training. It is not. It is missing infrastructure.
5. Describe what reinforcement looks like: call-level methodology scoring, same-day coaching, manager visibility. Not more classroom time. Real-time feedback on real calls.
6. Close with the reframe: the most expensive training investment is the one you have to make twice.

**Positioning lines to weave in:**
- "Training has a half-life. Most of it is 72 hours."
- "You did not buy bad training. You bought training without reinforcement."
- "The re-training tax is the cost of treating methodology like an event instead of an operating system."

**Do NOT:**
- Pitch OCC directly
- Mention pricing
- Claim OCC supports MEDDIC, SPIN, or Gap Selling as live modules
- Use the word "neuroscience"
- End with "Book a demo"

**Target audience:** CROs, VP Sales, Sales Enablement leaders at B2B companies with 5–50 reps who invested in methodology training.
