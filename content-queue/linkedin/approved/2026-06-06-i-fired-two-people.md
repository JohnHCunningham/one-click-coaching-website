I fired two people. Not because they couldn't sell. Not because they missed quota.

Because they didn't follow my path.

I got promoted from rep to manager. They handed me a book — From Selling to Managing. That was the training. One book.

I walked into the role believing my job was to find what was wrong and fix it. So I enforced my path. If a rep deviated from how I thought things should be done, I corrected it. Hard.

Two people lost their jobs. Not for performance. For doing it differently than I would have.

I regret both of those decisions. Not because the outcomes were wrong on paper. Because I never gave them a real chance.

The problem wasn't the reps. The problem was a manager who was never trained to manage.

I was 26. I'm 79 now. I still think about those two people.
