## Post Content

87% of sales training content is forgotten within 30 days.

Yet most organizations keep investing in more training, not better reinforcement.

The neuroscience is clear: behavior change requires three conditions - spaced repetition, contextual practice, and immediate feedback. But here's what most miss: the brain needs multiple encoding pathways to build lasting neural connections.

Training creates awareness. Practice creates competence. Reinforcement creates permanence.

The companies that crack this code don't run training events - they build learning systems. They embed micro-moments of reinforcement into daily workflows. They measure behavior change, not course completion.

The gap isn't in what salespeople learn. It's in what they retain, apply, and sustain.

What's your organization's reinforcement strategy beyond the initial training?
