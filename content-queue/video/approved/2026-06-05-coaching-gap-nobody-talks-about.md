TITLE: The Coaching Gap Nobody Talks About

HOOK: Twenty percent. That's how many managers your reps actually rate as effective coaches.

CONTEXT: We keep citing the wrong number. Managers spend less than 20% of their time coaching — everyone knows that stat. Here's the one nobody mentions: only 40% of those managers were ever trained to coach in the first place. That's not a performance problem. That's a design problem.

INSIGHT: When observers sit in on manager one-on-ones, real-time call review gets replaced by deal status updates. Role-play happens in fewer than 15% of sessions. The manager defaults to what got them promoted: pipeline, activity, forecast. Daily micro-coaching — five minutes, one behavior — drives more change than monthly hour-long sessions. But a manager with ten reps can't deliver that manually. The arithmetic doesn't work.

CLOSE: Who trained your managers to coach? If the answer is nobody — what did you expect to happen?
