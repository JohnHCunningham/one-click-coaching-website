## Final Revised Post

Your team may not have a coaching problem.

It may have a sampling problem.

A manager listens to one call.
Finds one issue.
Gives one note.

That is coaching.

But it is not pattern recognition.

The pattern usually shows up across calls.

Monday: the rep skips the upfront contract.
Wednesday: they ask shallow pain questions.
Friday: they rush the demo because the buyer sounds interested.

Then the manager reviews one call and coaches talk time.

The manager did nothing wrong.

They coached what they could see.

But methodology drift often lives in the calls nobody has time to inspect.

That is how good managers miss repeated behavior.

Not because they lack discipline.
Not because they do not care.
Not because the workshop failed.

Because sampling creates blind spots.

And blind spots become pipeline risk.

The question is not whether your managers are coaching.

The question is whether they can see enough calls to know the pattern.

One call creates a note.

A pattern creates the coaching that changes behavior.

## Suggested First Comment

Sampled coaching can feel responsible while still missing the real issue.

One reviewed call gives you a moment.
A sequence of calls gives you the behavior.

That is the difference between correcting a symptom and reinforcing the methodology.
