# LinkedIn Post 4 — 72 Hours / The Drift Window
# Schedule: Thu Jun 4, 4:30 PM ET
# Pillar: Training Drift / Cost of Drift
# Source: Research brief expansion — Ebbinghaus forgetting curve + training drift

Most sales training has a half-life of 72 hours.

Not because the training was bad.

Because the reinforcement loop was never built.

Here is what happens:

Day one after training: the team uses the new language.
Day seven: a few phrases remain.
Day thirty: pressure hits. The rep defaults.

Not to what they learned.
To what feels safe under scrutiny.

That is not a rep problem.

It is a system problem.

The workshop transferred knowledge.

Nobody built the repetition.

The question for any sales leader who just invested in methodology training:

What happens on day four?
Day twelve?
Day thirty-one?

If the answer is "the manager coaches when they can," the drift is already happening.
