# Outreach Strategy — One Click Coaching

**Purpose:** Generate qualified discovery calls through cold outreach, LinkedIn engagement, and email campaigns.

**Principle:** Insight over pitch. Pattern recognition over persuasion. Restraint over volume.

---

## Cold Email Framework

### The OCC Email Structure

**Subject line → Hook → Problem → Insight → Soft ask**

Every email follows this architecture. No exceptions.

### Subject Line Rules

**Length:** 3-7 words maximum
**Style:** Curiosity through restraint. Tension-based. No hype.

**The 4 Subject Line Formulas:**

1. **The Number**
   - "72 hours."
   - "37 calls. One pattern."
   - "$200k. No ROI."

2. **The Incomplete Statement**
   - "Your Sandler training has a half-life."
   - "The gap between training and Monday morning."
   - "Pressure reveals habit, not intention."

3. **The Provocation**
   - "More coaching isn't the problem."
   - "Your CRM isn't a coaching tool."
   - "Gong tells managers. Who tells reps?"

4. **The Question (Use Sparingly)**
   - "What happened after the Sandler rollout?"
   - "Where did the training ROI go?"

**Never:**
- Exclamation marks
- "Quick question"
- "Following up"
- "Just checking in"
- All caps
- Emojis

## Icebreaker System (Personalization Protocol)

**Rule:** Never send a template without personalization. Generic = ignored.

**The 3-Level Icebreaker Hierarchy:**

### Level 1: Deep Research (15-20 min per prospect)
**When to use:** High-value targets (VP of Sales at $50M+ company, Sandler franchisee, warm intro)

**What to reference:**
- Recent LinkedIn post (quote specific line)
- Podcast appearance (mention specific insight they shared)
- Blog post they wrote (tie OCC to their thesis)
- Company announcement (funding, product launch, training rollout)
- Mutual connection (not "we have a mutual connection" — name them and the context)

**Example:**
> "I saw your post last week about onboarding strong but new reps backsliding after 90 days. You wrote: 'They know the playbook. They just don't run it under pressure.' That's exactly the problem One Click Coaching solves."

### Level 2: Medium Research (5-10 min per prospect)
**When to use:** Mid-tier targets (Sales Director, Enablement Manager, qualified ICP)

**What to reference:**
- LinkedIn headline or About section
- Recent job change (congratulate, then tie to OCC use case)
- Company size/industry (show you understand their context)
- Tools they use (visible in LinkedIn profile or company tech stack)

**Example:**
> "I see you're leading sales enablement at {{Company}} — mid-market SaaS, 15-20 reps. That's exactly the profile where methodology drift hits hardest. Your reps are trained, but manual coaching doesn't scale at that size."

### Level 3: Light Research (2-3 min per prospect)
**When to use:** Volume outreach (ICP match but lower priority, testing new segments)

**What to reference:**
- Industry pain (generic but relevant)
- Recent company news (funding, leadership change)
- Methodology training visible on company website

**Example:**
> "Most B2B SaaS companies invest heavily in sales training but struggle with reinforcement. I'm guessing {{Company}} has felt that pain too."

**Minimum bar:** At least Level 3 personalization. Never send fully generic.

## LinkedIn Outreach

### Connection Request (50 Characters Max)

**Template:**
> "Saw your post on {{topic}} — great insight."

**Or:**
> "{{Mutual connection}} suggested we connect."

**Never:**
- "I'd love to connect"
- "Let's grow our networks"
- Generic requests with no context

### First Message After Connection (Within 24 Hours)

**Template:**
```
{{FirstName}},

Thanks for connecting. I saw your post about {{specific pain}}.

That's the exact problem One Click Coaching solves — we score every call against your methodology and fire same-day feedback to reps so training actually sticks.

Not pitching. Just thought it might be relevant given what you're working on.

If you're curious, happy to share what the scoring output looks like. 20 minutes, no pressure.
```

**Key principle:** Soft ask. Never "book a demo." Always "happy to show you."

## Email Sequence Structure

### Sequence 1: Cold Outreach (5 Emails Over 14 Days)

**Email 1 (Day 0):** Core insight email (Template 1, 2, 3, or 4)
- **Goal:** Get reply or meeting booked
- **CTA:** "Worth 20 minutes to see what this looks like?"

**Email 2 (Day 3):** Pattern recognition follow-up
- **Goal:** Reframe the problem from a different angle
- **Template:**
```
{{FirstName}},

Following up on my note from Tuesday.

Here's the pattern I see across every sales team we work with:
- Reps are trained on the methodology
- Managers don't have time to coach every call
- Performance plateaus 90 days after training

One Click Coaching closes that gap. Same-day feedback, every rep, every call.

Want to see what this looks like in practice?
```

**Email 3 (Day 7):** Social proof + outcome
- **Goal:** Build credibility with evidence
- **Template:**
```
{{FirstName}},

We analyzed 10,000 sales calls and found that 87% of methodology training is forgotten within 30 days without same-day reinforcement.

That's not a motivation problem. It's a system problem.

One Click Coaching is the system. Worth 20 minutes?
```

**Email 4 (Day 10):** Contrarian take
- **Goal:** Challenge assumptions, create cognitive dissonance
- **Template:**
```
{{FirstName}},

Most sales leaders think the solution to inconsistent performance is more training.

It's not. It's reinforcement.

Your reps already know what to do. They just don't do it under pressure. One Click Coaching reinforces the methodology at the moment it matters — the same day the call happened.

Curious if this resonates?
```

**Email 5 (Day 14):** Break-up email
- **Goal:** Create urgency through scarcity
- **Template:**
```
{{FirstName}},

I'll assume this isn't a priority right now and stop reaching out.

If that changes — or if you're just heads-down and want to revisit later — happy to show you what One Click Coaching looks like in practice.

Either way, good luck with the team.
```

**Key principle:** If no reply after Email 5, stop. Do not become spam.

## Subject Line A/B Testing

**Test these variables:**
- Length (3 words vs 7 words)
- Style (number vs statement vs provocation)
- Specificity (generic "sales training" vs specific "Sandler training")
- Punctuation (period vs no punctuation vs question mark)

**Track:**
- Open rate (subject line effectiveness)
- Reply rate (email body effectiveness)
- Meeting booked rate (offer effectiveness)

**Sample size:** Minimum 50 emails per variant before declaring winner.

**Update this section quarterly with winning subject lines.**

## Outreach Channels (Ranked by Effectiveness)

### 1. Warm Intro (Highest Conversion)
**Source:** Mutual connection, existing customer, Sandler franchisee
**Conversion rate:** 40-60% → discovery call
**Process:**
1. Ask introducer to send intro email (you draft it)
2. Follow up within 24 hours
3. Book meeting in first email (no sequence needed)

**Intro email template (for introducer to send):**
```
{{FirstName}},

I wanted to introduce you to {{YourName}}, founder of One Click Coaching.

{{Company}} uses OCC to reinforce our Sandler training — scores every call, fires same-day feedback to reps, escalates recurring weaknesses to managers. Training ROI finally stuck.

Given your recent {{methodology}} rollout, thought it might be relevant.

I'll let {{YourName}} take it from here.
```

### 2. LinkedIn DM (After Connection)
**Conversion rate:** 15-25% → discovery call
**Process:**
1. Send connection request with context
2. Wait for acceptance
3. Send soft-ask message within 24 hours (see template above)

---

### 3. Cold Email (Personalized)
**Conversion rate:** 5-10% → discovery call
**Process:**
1. Research prospect (Icebreaker System)
2. Send Email 1 from sequence
3. Follow up per sequence structure (Emails 2-5)

---

### 4. LinkedIn Comment (Nurture Play)
**Conversion rate:** 2-5% → discovery call (long-term)
**Process:**
1. Comment on prospect's LinkedIn posts (genuinely add value, no pitch)
2. Build familiarity over 2-3 months
3. Send connection request with context
4. Soft-ask DM after connection

---

### 5. Cold LinkedIn DM (No Connection)
**Conversion rate:** 1-3% → discovery call
**Not recommended unless high-value target and warm intro impossible.**

## Email Hygiene (Avoid Spam Folder)

**Rules:**
- Warm up new email domain (use Lemwarm, Mailreach, etc.)
- Send < 50 emails/day from single address
- Personalize every email (no mail merge blasts)
- No attachments in first email
- No link shorteners (bit.ly, etc.)
- No ALL CAPS
- No excessive punctuation!!!
- Use plain text (not HTML templates with images)
- Include unsubscribe option if sending volume

**Tools:**
- **Mail-tester.com** — Test spam score before sending
- **Hunter.io** — Verify email addresses before sending
- **Lemwarm** — Email warm-up service

## What NOT to Do

**Never:**
- ❌ Send generic templates with no personalization
- ❌ Pitch in the connection request
- ❌ Follow up more than 5 times (becomes spam)
- ❌ Use fake urgency ("limited spots available")
- ❌ Lie about mutual connections or research
- ❌ Send unsolicited calendar invites
- ❌ Use "just following up" or "bumping this to the top"
- ❌ Apologize for reaching out ("Sorry to bother you")
- ❌ Use exclamation marks in subject lines
- ❌ Send Monday mornings (inbox overload) or Friday afternoons (ignored)

**Best send times:**
- Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am or 2-4pm (prospect's local time)
