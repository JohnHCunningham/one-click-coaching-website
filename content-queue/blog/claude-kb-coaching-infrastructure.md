7. **Body:** ~800-1200 words. Scene → data → mechanism → implication. No fluff.

## Voice Rules

- Direct, opinionated, peer-to-peer. Not "thought leadership" — real leadership.
- Short paragraphs. Single-sentence paragraphs are fine.
- No AI-smooth language. No "in today's fast-paced business environment."
- No "we believe" or "at One Click Coaching." The blog is by John Cunningham, not the company.
- Data is earned, not dropped. Every stat has a source or a mechanism behind it.
- End with an image or a question more often than a lesson.
- The knowing-doing gap is the central lens.

## Banned Words & Phrases

- "In today's fast-paced business environment"
- "Unlock your team's potential"
- "Game-changer" / "revolutionary" / "disruptive"
- "Leverage" (the verb)
- "Deep dive"
- "Best-in-class" / "world-class"
- "Synergy" / "ecosystem" (unless literally about ecosystems)
- "Empower" (overused — use "equip" or "arm" if needed)
- "Double down"
- Any sentence starting with "Imagine..." or "Picture this..."

## THIS POST'S ANGLE: Coaching Infrastructure

**Hook:** "Every high-stakes discipline on earth built coaching into the workflow. Except yours."

**Core Argument:**
- Sports: every athlete has multiple coaches, video review after every performance
- Medicine: grand rounds, case review, peer observation — coaching is embedded in process
- Aviation: checklists, simulators, mandatory debriefs after every flight
- Military: after-action reviews are non-negotiable, not optional
- Sales: a manager and a calendar invite. Maybe. Coaching is bolted on, not built in.

**The contrast:** In disciplines where lives are at stake, coaching isn't a program — it's infrastructure. Sales, where millions of dollars ride on individual conversations, has no embedded feedback loop. No infrastructure. No automatic review. Just hoping managers find the time.

**Numbers to use:**
- Sports coaching ratios: 1:5 to 1:10 in pro sports vs 1:8 to 1:12 in B2B sales
- Medical residency: observed practice with immediate correction — hundreds of feedback touches
- Aviation debrief protocol: every flight, every time
- Sales: maybe 2 calls reviewed per rep per month. Maybe.

**The mechanism:** Coaching infrastructure means every performance gets reviewed — automatically. Humans review the exceptions, not the routine. The system catches drift before it hardens.

**Landing:** The systems exist now. AI has made coaching infrastructure possible at a price point that works for 5-50 rep teams. Companies that build the infrastructure layer will have a structural advantage. The question is who builds first.
