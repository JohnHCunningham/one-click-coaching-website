## Quick Start: The Sandler Submarine

### What is the Sandler Submarine?

The Sandler Submarine is a **7-step selling system** that qualifies prospects BEFORE you pitch. Most reps pitch too early and lose leverage. Sandler flips this: you qualify first, present last.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SANDLER SUBMARINE: 7 Compartments (All Must Be Sealed)    │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  1. Bonding & Rapport    → Build trust                     │
│  2. Up-Front Contract    → Set expectations                │
│  3. Pain                 → Dig deep (7 levels)             │
│  4. Budget               → Normalize money                 │
│  5. Decision             → Map WHO/HOW/WHEN                │
│  6. Fulfillment          → Present solution (only now!)    │
│  7. Post-Sell            → Prevent buyer's remorse         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Core Principles

1. **Don't spill your candy in the lobby** - Never pitch before qualifying
2. **You can't want it more than they do** - Test commitment, walk away if lukewarm
3. **Equal business stature** - You're peers, not subordinate
4. **Make "no" safe** - Eliminate maybes
5. **Reverse questions** - Answer with questions when they ask too early
6. **Stay in pain** - Go 6-7 levels deep before moving on
7. **Prospect's system always wins** - Adapt to their buying process

### The #1 Mistake Reps Make

**Spilling candy in the lobby** - giving away expertise before qualifying.

❌ **Bad:** Prospect asks "How does it work?" in minute 3 → Rep launches into demo → Prospect says "Let me think about it"

✅ **Good:** Prospect asks "How does it work?" → Rep reverses: "Good question - what specifically are you trying to solve that's driving that question?" → Stays in pain

## The 7 Compartments (Detailed)

### Compartment 1: Bonding & Rapport

**Objective:** Create trust and emotional safety for candid conversation.

**Why it matters:** Without rapport, prospects guard the truth. You get surface-level answers.

**Key behaviors:**
- Show genuine curiosity about their world
- Listen more than you talk (30-40% talk time)
- Use equality language: "This may or may not be a fit, and that's okay"

**Language patterns:**
```
✅ "Out of curiosity, how did you end up responsible for [their area]?"
✅ "Help me understand your situation..."
✅ "Tell me a bit about your world..."

❌ "So, how many reps do you have?" (Too abrupt)
❌ "Let me ask you some questions..." (Interrogation mode)
```

**Practice drill:**
- Next 5 calls: Start with "Out of curiosity..." and see how they open up
- Aim for 60%+ prospect talk time in first 5 minutes

### Compartment 2: Up-Front Contract

**Objective:** Set clear expectations (time, agenda, that "no" is okay) BEFORE diving in.

**Why it matters:** Without a contract, prospects feel ambushed. They're thinking "How long is this? Can I say no?" instead of listening.

**Template:**
> "So that we respect your time, here's what I'd like to do: [AGENDA]. We have [TIME]. If we get to the end and it's not a fit, are you comfortable telling me no so we don't create maybes? Fair enough?"

**Language patterns:**
```
✅ "I have a few questions to see if we're even a fit. Fair enough?"
✅ "If this isn't right, are you comfortable saying no?"
✅ "We said 15 minutes - does that still work?"

❌ [Jumps straight into questions without setting expectations]
❌ "This will only take a minute..." [then goes 20 minutes]
```

**Practice drill:**
- Script your up-front contract word-for-word
- Memorize it
- Use it on EVERY call for 2 weeks

### Compartment 3: Pain (The Pain Funnel)

**Objective:** Go from surface symptoms → deep business impact → personal consequences → commitment.

**Why it matters:** Surface pain doesn't create urgency. Deep pain (level 6-7) makes them say "I need to fix this NOW."

**The 7-Level Pain Funnel:**

```
Level 1: SURFACE
├─ "Tell me more about that."
├─ "Can you give me an example?"
└─ "What does that look like day-to-day?"

Level 2: DURATION
├─ "How long has that been happening?"
└─ "When did you first notice this?"

Level 3: IMPACT (Business)
├─ "What does that cost the business?"
├─ "What does that do to your pipeline?"
└─ "How many deals does that cost you?"

Level 4: IMPACT (Personal)
├─ "How does that affect YOU personally?"
├─ "How does that make you feel?"
└─ "What does that mean for you?"

Level 5: CONSEQUENCES
├─ "What happens if nothing changes?"
├─ "What does that mean for your team?"
└─ "Where will you be in 6 months if this doesn't get fixed?"

Level 6: GAVE UP?
├─ "Have you given up trying to fix this?"
└─ "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"

Level 7: WHY NOW?
├─ "Why fix it now versus six months from now?"
├─ "What's different now that makes this urgent?"
└─ "Why now?"
```

**Example of going deep:**

```
Rep: "What challenges are you facing with your sales team?"
Prospect: "Reps aren't using the methodology we trained them on."

Rep: "Tell me more about that." [Level 1: Surface]
Prospect: "We spent $15k per rep on Sandler training. They were great for a month. Now they're back to old habits."

Rep: "How long has that been happening?" [Level 2: Duration]
Prospect: "About 90 days after training."

Rep: "What's that costing you?" [Level 3: Impact]
Prospect: "We're seeing 3-4 deals stall every quarter because they're not qualifying properly."

Rep: "How does that affect YOU personally?" [Level 4: Personal]
Prospect: "My VP is asking why we invested $150k in training if nothing's changed. I'm on the hook."

Rep: "What happens if nothing changes?" [Level 5: Consequences]
Prospect: "Honestly? I might not have a job in 6 months if I can't prove ROI."

Rep: "Have you given up trying to fix this?" [Level 6: Gave up?]
Prospect: "Kind of. I don't know what else to do. We've tried weekly coaching but managers don't have time."

Rep: "Why fix it now versus waiting another quarter?" [Level 7: Why now?]
Prospect: "Board meeting in 60 days. If I don't show improvement, they'll cut my budget."
```

**Critical rule: STAY IN PAIN**
- Don't jump to solution
- Don't say "We can help with that!"
- Ask 6-7 pain questions in a row
- Tolerate silence (3-5 seconds after they answer)

**Advanced: The Pendulum Technique**

After uncovering deep pain (8/10), PULL BACK with negative reverse:

```
✅ "Honestly, if it's only 3-4 deals a quarter, this might not be worth changing."
✅ "Maybe sticking with your current approach is actually the best move."
✅ "I'm not sure we're the right fit - this seems pretty minor."
```

**Why this works:** When you pull back, their brain says "Wait, I DO have a problem!" They lean in. This is the Pendulum: pull back before pushing forward.

**Practice drill:**
- Next call: Ask 7 pain questions in a row
- Go all the way to level 7 before moving on
- Try the Pendulum technique after uncovering 8/10 pain

### Compartment 4: Budget

**Objective:** Normalize money talk. Understand ability AND willingness to invest.

**Why it matters:** You'll waste weeks on deals that can't afford you. Budget is a qualifier, not a closer.

**How to normalize money:**
```
✅ "Can we talk a bit about budget so we both know whether this makes sense?"
✅ "How do you usually decide what's worth investing in for this kind of problem?"
✅ "What were you thinking of investing to solve this?"

❌ [Skips budget, hopes they'll figure it out later]
❌ "We'll talk pricing after the demo"
```

**If they ask price BEFORE pain (reversing):**
```
Prospect: "How much does this cost?"

❌ Bad: "It's $50 per rep per month." [Spills candy]

✅ Good: "Fair question - it varies depending on what you need. Can we talk about what you're trying to solve first so I can give you a meaningful range?"

✅ Better: "Good question. Before I answer that - what were YOU thinking of investing to solve [their pain]?"
```

**If budget is vague:**
```
Prospect: "We'll find budget if it makes sense."

❌ Bad: "Great!" [Accepts vague answer]

✅ Good: "'We'll find budget' - I've heard that before and it usually means there's no budget. Should we reconnect when budget is actually allocated?"

✅ Better: "So there's no budget allocated yet - is that right? Who would need to approve this and what's that process?"
```

**Disqualify if no budget:**
```
✅ "If $500/month isn't in the budget, let's not waste your time with a demo."
✅ "Sounds like budget isn't allocated yet - should we reconnect when it is?"
```

**Practice drill:**
- On next 3 calls, say: "Can we talk about budget so we both know if this makes sense?"
- If they ask price early, reverse with: "What were YOU thinking of investing?"

### Compartment 5: Decision

**Objective:** Understand WHO decides, HOW they decide, WHEN they decide.

**Why it matters:** Surprise decision makers kill deals. Map the committee BEFORE presenting.

**ALWAYS ask these 3 questions:**

1. **WHO decides?**
```
✅ "Besides you, who else will be involved in deciding whether we move forward?"
✅ "Who else has to sign off on this?"
✅ "Talk me through who's in the room when this gets decided."
```

2. **HOW do they decide?**
```
✅ "Walk me through how decisions like this usually get made at your company."
✅ "What does a 'yes' actually look like procedurally?"
✅ "What has to happen between now and a signed contract?"
```

3. **WHEN will they decide?**
```
✅ "When are you looking to have this decided?"
✅ "Is that timeline real or are we just hoping?"
✅ "What happens if this doesn't get decided by then?"
```

**Advanced: "What Then?" Questions**

After they describe the decision process, ask "What then?" 3-4 times to flush roadblocks:

```
Rep: "So you present to your VP... what then?"
Prospect: "She'll want to see ROI data."

Rep: "And then what happens?"
Prospect: "Then it goes to finance for approval."

Rep: "What then?"
Prospect: "Well, finance will want to see if we have budget this quarter..."

Rep: "And if there's no budget this quarter?"
Prospect: "Then it gets pushed to next quarter."

→ You just found the real roadblock.
```

**Prospect's System Always Wins**

Don't fight their buying process. Adapt to it.

```
❌ Bad: "We don't usually do trials." [Fighting their system]

✅ Good: "Help me understand how trials typically work at your company. Walk me through your process."
```

**Practice drill:**
- On EVERY call, ask "Besides you, who else is involved?"
- After they describe the process, ask "What then?" 4 times

### Compartment 6: Fulfillment

**Objective:** Present solution ONLY after qualifying. Tie features to their specific pains.

**Why it matters:** If you present before qualifying, you have no leverage. They got your expertise for free.

**Checklist before presenting:**
```
□ Bonding & Rapport: Do they trust you?
□ Up-Front Contract: Are expectations set?
□ Pain: Did you go 6-7 levels deep?
□ Budget: Do you know if they can afford it?
□ Decision: Do you know who decides and how?

→ ONLY THEN present solution
```

**How to present:**

1. **Reference THEIR pains** (not generic benefits)
```
❌ Bad: "We have AI that scores calls and provides coaching."

✅ Good: "You mentioned reps revert to old habits after training - we solve that by scoring EVERY call so you catch drift before deals stall. Does that address what you were talking about?"
```

2. **Keep it conversational** (not a monologue)
- Present for 2-3 minutes MAX
- Then check in: "How does this land?"
- Make it a dialogue, not a pitch

3. **Test alignment throughout**
```
✅ "How does this compare to what you were hoping to see?"
✅ "Does this address what you mentioned earlier about [pain]?"
✅ "What questions do you have so far?"
✅ "I'm sensing some hesitation - what's not sitting right?"
```

**Template:**
> "Based on what you've shared - [RECAP THEIR PAIN] - here's how we'd solve that: [FEATURE TIED TO THAT PAIN]. Does that address what you were talking about?"

**Practice drill:**
- Don't present ANYTHING until compartments 1-5 are sealed
- Before presenting any feature, say "You mentioned [their pain]..."
- Set a timer: Present for 2 minutes, then ask "How does this land?"

---

### Compartment 7: Post-Sell

**Objective:** Prevent buyer's remorse. Flush objections BEFORE they happen.

**Why it matters:** Deals die AFTER the yes. They ghost you because they got cold feet and you didn't address it.

**After they agree, do these 3 things:**

1. **Recap agreement**
```
✅ "Great! Just to recap, we've agreed on [X, Y, Z]. Does that still feel right?"
✅ "Let me make sure I have this right: [RECAP]. Sound good?"
```

2. **Flush internal pushback**
```
✅ "Who might push back on this internally?"
✅ "How do you think finance will react when they see this?"
✅ "What will you say when your VP asks why now?"
✅ "Who's going to be skeptical and why?"
```

3. **Set success criteria & failure signals**
```
✅ "What does success look like 90 days from now?"
✅ "What's the first sign this isn't going as planned?"
✅ "What would make you regret this decision?"
```

**Advanced: Negative Reverse at Stall Points ONLY**

If the deal STALLS (goes dark for 2+ weeks, timeline slips), use negative reverse:

```
✅ "Should we just take this off the table for now?"
✅ "Honestly, it feels like this may not be the right time. Should we reconnect when it's more urgent?"
✅ "Maybe we should pause this until you have more clarity."
```

**⚠️ WARNING:** Only use negative reverse when deals STALL. Not during active conversation.

**Practice drill:**
- After EVERY yes, ask "Who might push back internally?"
- Then ask: "What will you say to them?" (Coach them through it)

## Role Play Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Prospect Asks "How Does It Work?" Too Early

**Setup:** You're 3 minutes into the call. You've done bonding and up-front contract. You ask your first pain question.

**Prospect:** "Before we go further, can you just tell me how it works?"

**Your turn:** How do you respond?

<details>
<summary>Click to see GOOD response</summary>

**Rep:** "Good question - and I'm happy to show you. But before I do, can we talk about what specifically you're trying to solve? That way I can show you the parts that actually matter to you instead of a generic walkthrough. Fair enough?"

**Why this works:**
- You didn't say no (which feels rude)
- You reversed back to pain
- You framed it as beneficial to THEM
- You got permission to continue qualifying

</details>

<summary>Click to see BAD response</summary>

**Rep:** "Oh great question! So we have an AI that listens to every call and scores it against your methodology. Then we provide same-day coaching so reps don't drift. We integrate with Gong and Salesforce..."

**Why this fails:**
- Spilled your candy
- Gave away expertise before qualifying
- Now they can say "Let me think about it" and shop you

## Common Objections & Reversing

### "We're already working with [competitor]"

❌ **Bad:** "Well, here's why we're better..."

✅ **Good:** "That's great - what made you take this call if you're already working with them?"

✅ **Better:** "Interesting. Out of curiosity, if things were working perfectly with [competitor], why are we talking?"

---

### "We don't have budget right now"

❌ **Bad:** "No problem, I can follow up next quarter."

✅ **Good:** "Fair enough. Out of curiosity - if budget weren't an issue, would this solve [their pain]?"

✅ **Better:** "Got it. So if there's no budget, should we take this off the table for now? Or is there a path to getting budget allocated if the ROI makes sense?"

---

### "Can you send me some information?"

❌ **Bad:** "Sure, I'll send you a deck!" [sends deck, gets ghosted]

✅ **Good:** "Happy to - what specifically were you hoping to see in that information?"

✅ **Better:** "I could send you something generic, but can we talk about what you're solving first so I send you something relevant? Otherwise it's just more noise in your inbox."

---

### "Your price is too high"

❌ **Bad:** "Well, let me see what I can do..." [discounts]

✅ **Good:** "Too high compared to what?"

✅ **Better:** "Fair enough. Out of curiosity - you mentioned [their pain] is costing you [X]. If we solve that, what's the ROI?"

---

### "I need to run this by my team"

❌ **Bad:** "Great! Let me know what they say."

✅ **Good:** "Makes sense. Who specifically will you be talking to?"

✅ **Better:** "Got it. Walk me through that conversation - who's in the room, what will you say, and what objections do you expect?"

## Manager Playbook

### How to Use One Click Coaching with Your Team

**Week 1: Onboarding**
1. Share these training materials with your team
2. Have them read the 7 Compartments section
3. Role play scenarios together
4. Set expectations: OCC will score every call and deliver same-day coaching

**Week 2-4: Foundation**
1. Focus on the Big 3:
   - ✅ Always set up-front contract
   - ✅ Never spill candy in lobby (no pitching before pain)
   - ✅ Use the Pain Funnel (6-7 levels deep)
2. Review OCC coaching daily with each rep
3. Track improvement on these 3 metrics

**Week 5-8: Refinement**
1. Layer in advanced techniques:
   - Reversing
   - Pendulum technique
   - "What then?" questions
2. Use OCC scoring to identify patterns
3. Celebrate improvements

**Week 9+: Mastery**
1. Track methodology adherence vs close rates
2. Identify which compartments correlate with closed deals
3. Use OCC data to prove ROI to leadership

---

### How to Run a Weekly Coaching Session

**15-Minute 1:1 Format:**

1. **Review OCC scores** (5 min)
   - Pull up rep's calls from past week
   - Show compartment scores
   - Identify biggest gap (e.g., "You skipped up-front contract on 4 out of 5 calls")

2. **Pick ONE focus area** (3 min)
   - Don't overwhelm with everything
   - Pick the highest-impact gap
   - Example: "This week, focus on setting up-front contract on EVERY call"

3. **Role play** (5 min)
   - Practice the specific scenario
   - Use real examples from their calls
   - Rep practices, you give feedback

4. **Set practice assignment** (2 min)
   - "Next 5 calls, I want you to set up-front contract every time. We'll review Friday."
   - Track in OCC

### What to Look For in OCC Reports

**High-Priority Flags:**
- 🚨 Spilling candy in lobby (pitching before pain)
- 🚨 Skipping up-front contract
- 🚨 Staying at surface pain (not going deep)
- 🚨 Assuming they're the decision maker

**Medium-Priority Flags:**
- ⚠️ Not reversing pricing questions
- ⚠️ Accepting vague budget answers
- ⚠️ Monologuing in fulfillment

**Opportunity for Excellence:**
- ✨ Could use Pendulum technique
- ✨ Could use "What then?" to flush roadblocks
- ✨ Could establish equal business stature

---

### How to Prove ROI to Leadership

**Track these metrics in OCC:**

1. **Methodology Adherence Score** (0-100)
   - Average compartment score across all calls
   - Track trend over time
   - Goal: 70+ (passing), 85+ (excellent)

2. **Improvement Rate**
   - % of reps improving week-over-week
   - Example: "8 out of 10 reps improved their Pain Funnel score by 15+ points"

3. **Correlation to Close Rates**
   - Close rate for calls scoring 70+ vs under 70
   - Example: "Calls scoring 80+ close at 40%. Calls under 60 close at 12%."

4. **ROI Calculation**
   - Methodology adherence ↑ 25% → Close rate ↑ 15% → Revenue ↑ $X
   - OCC cost: $500/month → ROI: [Calculate]

**Present to leadership quarterly:**
- "Before OCC: 45% methodology adherence, 22% close rate"
- "After 90 days: 78% methodology adherence, 34% close rate"
- "Revenue impact: +$XYZ"

## Quick Wins: Start Here

**If you do nothing else, do these 3 things:**

### 1. Set an Up-Front Contract (Every Call)
> "I have a few questions to see if we're even a fit. Fair enough? If we get to the end and it's not a fit, are you comfortable telling me no?"

**Why:** Makes "no" safe, reduces resistance, sets expectations

---

### 2. Don't Spill Your Candy in the Lobby
> When they ask "How does it work?" → REVERSE: "What specifically are you trying to solve?"

**Why:** Preserves leverage, ensures you qualify before pitching

---

### 3. Use the Pain Funnel (Go 6-7 Levels Deep)
> Surface → Duration → Impact → Personal → Consequences → Gave up? → Why now?

**Why:** Deep pain creates urgency, surface pain doesn't
