Most sales training doesn't fail.

It gets abandoned.

I've spent enough time around sales leaders to see the pattern clearly. A team goes through a methodology — Challenger, Sandler, MEDDIC, whatever it is. For about three weeks, the framework shows up in conversations. Questions get sharper. Deals get handled differently.

Then pipeline pressure returns.

The manager who was supposed to reinforce goes back to deal reviews. The rep who started strong reverts to what feels natural under stress. Within 90 days, the training investment is invisible — not because the training was weak, but because nothing was built to sustain it.

The same momentum loss Andrea and Tony described in deals happens to methodology adoption. The story doesn't survive the pressure.

The industry talks endlessly about better training. Very few talk about what happens after.
