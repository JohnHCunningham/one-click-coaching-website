## OCC Advisor Analysis

### What Is Working
- **Scene opening is strong.** "Monday morning. Pipeline review." — immediate recognition for the audience. This is their life.
- **"Two versions of your pipeline"** is a sticky frame. The split between what the CRM shows and what actually happened is the central tension, and it's introduced cleanly.
- **"The CRM doesn't care"** is a weighted, memorable refrain. Used twice — not overused, lands both times.
- **Three-part cost breakdown** (forecast accuracy → pipeline integrity → methodology decay) is structurally sound and escalates naturally.
- **"Measurement parity"** is a strong original concept that could become a positioning pillar.
- **Methodology-agnostic language throughout.** "Qualification framework," "discovery structure," "budget authority" — works for Sandler, MEDDIC, SPIN, Challenger, Gap equally.
- **Soft CTA lands well.** "It's about making the pipeline report honest" before the CTA link — earned, not forced.

### What Could Be Stronger
- **The "Why This Gap Persists" section is analytically correct but reads slightly flat.** The "structural asymmetry" point is good but could be sharpened with one concrete sentence about what a rep actually experiences in a pipeline review when they *did* run proper qualification but didn't advance the stage versus the rep who advanced without it. Show the incentive, don't just describe it.
- **One deal post-mortem would add texture.** A brief, anonymized example of a deal that "slipped" at proposal because qualification was never done — what the CRM showed vs. what the call revealed. One paragraph. Adds specificity without bloating.
- **Close could anchor harder.** The current close is solid but epistrophe (repeated ending) at the end of the last 3-4 lines would make it linger. The word "honest" is used — could echo it: "A deal is only as real as the qualification that supports it. A forecast is only as honest as the calls behind it."

### Rhetorical Devices Used (Count: 6)
1. **Metaphor:** "Pipeline mirage" throughout — the central organizing metaphor
2. **Antithesis:** "CRM stages measure what a rep clicked. They do not measure what a rep executed."
3. **Tricolon:** Three costs (forecast accuracy, pipeline integrity, methodology decay)
4. **Anaphora:** "The CRM doesn't care. / It records the stage change. It updates the probability. It colors the dashboard green."
5. **Polyptoton:** measure/measured/measurement throughout
6. **Analogy:** Implicit — the pipeline mirage functions like a GPS showing arrival when you're still in the parking lot

Suggested additions for light edit:
- Add one deal post-mortem paragraph in "Why This Gap Persists" section
- Strengthen close with epistrophe on "honest"
- Minor: tighten "The Fix Is Measurement Parity" section title for SEO — "Closing the Gap" works, but "From Pipeline Stages to Methodology Scores" might be more descriptive
