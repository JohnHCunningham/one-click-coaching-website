## Post Content

87% of training content is forgotten within 30 days.

Yet organizations keep investing in the same cycle: Big training event. High energy. Rapid decay.

The neuroscience is clear: Our brains are designed to forget. Without reinforcement, even the most engaging content becomes cognitive debris within weeks.

Here's what the research shows about lasting behavior change:

• Spaced repetition beats intensive sessions 3:1
• Micro-learning creates stronger neural pathways than marathon sessions
• Application within 24 hours increases retention by 400%

The problem isn't your training content. The problem isn't your training delivery. The problem is treating learning like an event instead of a system.

Top performers don't attend more training—they practice more deliberately. They reinforce more consistently. They apply more immediately.

The gap between knowing and doing isn't about knowledge transfer. It's about behavior architecture.

What if your next training investment focused less on what people learn and more on what they actually change?
