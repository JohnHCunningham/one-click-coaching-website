# Final Revised Post

The CRM says the stage changed.

The call says the methodology disappeared.

You have seen this.

A deal moves from Discovery to Demo.
The rep logged the meeting.
Attached the deck.
Updated the stage.

The CRM looks clean.

But on the call, the rep skipped the upfront contract.
Accepted surface pain.
Rushed into features the buyer did not ask for.
Left with a next step that sounded clear, but was not real.

The stage moved.

The methodology did not.

That is the danger of managing only what gets entered.

CRM hygiene can create a false positive.
It can make activity look like execution.
It can make rep optimism look like buyer commitment.

But methodology adoption is not a field in the CRM.

It is a behavior on the call.

And behavior, unchecked, drifts toward what feels easiest under pressure — not what was taught in the workshop.

The question is not whether your team updates the CRM.

It is whether the CRM reflects real buyer commitment or just a rep moving the deal forward in the system.

That is where training drift hides.
