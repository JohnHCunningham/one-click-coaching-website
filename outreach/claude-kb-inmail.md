# OCC InMail — Claude Project KB

## Success Criteria — Verify Before Delivering

Your InMail passes when ALL of these are true:
1. ✅ Subject line ≤ 50 characters. Required — InMail needs a subject.
2. ✅ Body ≤ 300 characters. LinkedIn's InMail body limit.
3. ✅ Opens with context: who you are, why you're writing. Not a pitch.
4. ✅ One specific observation about their work. Proof you looked at their profile.
5. ✅ Soft close. "Worth a conversation?" not "Let's schedule a call."
6. ✅ Zero OCC mentions unless they're already a Sandler partner/franchisee (then "white-label diagnostics layer" is fine).
7. ✅ No invented biographical details. Only what's in their Sales Nav profile.

If the InMail fails any criterion, rewrite before delivering.

---

## What You Write

LinkedIn InMail messages for 3rd-degree connections who can't receive a free connection request. Sent via Sales Navigator.

## InMail Rules

- **InMail credits are limited.** Use sparingly. Every InMail costs a credit.
- **Subject line is required.** Unlike connection notes, InMail has a subject field.
- **No sign-off.** No "Regards," "Best," "John Cunningham."
- **Never pitch.** This is a handshake, not a sale.

## InMail Patterns

### Pattern A: Sandler Franchisee / Partner
Subject: "Sandler reinforcement layer"
"Hi [Name] — I've built a white-label diagnostics platform that scores rep calls against the Sandler framework. It extends what franchisees install between sessions — automated coaching cards, manager approval, same-day feedback. Non-competitive by design. Worth comparing notes?"

### Pattern B: VP Sales (cold)
Subject: "The 4% coaching gap"
"Hi [Name] — [Company]'s growth caught my attention. Most sales teams run ~480 calls/month with managers reviewing ~20. That 4% sampling rate means 96% of calls generate zero feedback. I built something that closes that gap — methodology-aware call scoring, automated coaching. Worth 20 minutes to show you?"

### Pattern C: Sales Enablement Leader
Subject: "Training ROI question"
"Hi [Name] — you can measure training attendance. You can measure cert scores. If your board asked whether the methodology is showing up on live calls — what would you point to? Most enablement leaders I talk to have nothing. I work on that specific measurement gap. Worth a conversation?"

## When to Use

- 3rd-degree connections (no free connection request available)
- High-value targets where the personalization effort pays off
- Someone whose profile is unusually detailed — gives you material to reference

## When NOT to Use

- 2nd-degree connections (use free connection request + note instead)
- Anyone you can reach through a mutual connection for an introduction
- Bulk outreach — save credits for the highest-signal targets
