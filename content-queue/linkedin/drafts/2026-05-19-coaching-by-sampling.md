## Metadata

- Content pillar: Manager Capacity / Cost of Drift
- Target persona: VP Sales, Sales Managers, Sales Enablement leaders, CROs at B2B companies with 5–50 sales reps
- Hook type: Provocation / mechanism
- Angle selected: Manager-led coaching breaks when it becomes sampling. Even good managers only hear fragments of rep behavior, so recurring methodology drift hides between reviewed calls.

## Post Draft

Most sales teams are not being coached.

They are being sampled.

A manager listens to one call.
Finds one issue.
Gives one note.

Useful.

But incomplete.

Because the real pattern is rarely visible in one call.

A rep skips the upfront contract on Monday.
Asks shallow pain questions on Wednesday.
Rushes the demo on Friday.
Then gets coached on talk time because that happened to be the call someone reviewed.

The manager did nothing wrong.

They coached what they could see.

But methodology drift lives in the calls nobody has time to inspect.

That is why good managers still miss repeated behavior.

Not because they lack discipline.
Not because they do not care.
Not because the training failed in the room.

Because sampling creates blind spots.

And blind spots become pipeline risk.

The next question for sales leaders is not, “are we coaching?”

It is, “are we seeing enough behavior to know what needs coaching?”

## Suggested First Comment

The uncomfortable part is that sampled coaching can feel responsible while still missing the pattern.

One call gives a moment.
A sequence of calls gives the behavior.

That distinction matters when you are trying to make methodology training stick.

## Why This Should Appeal To The Audience

Sales leaders already believe coaching matters, but they feel the bandwidth problem every week. This post reframes the issue from manager effort to visibility: even responsible coaching can miss recurring methodology drift if it only samples calls. That gives VP Sales and Sales Enablement leaders a useful way to explain why training reinforcement requires a more systematic loop.
