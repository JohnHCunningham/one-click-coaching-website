## Post Content

72 hours.

That's how long most sales training survives in the wild before old habits reclaim their territory.

Yet organizations spend millions on elaborate training programs, expecting permanent behavior change from temporary interventions. The science tells a different story.

Neuroscientist Dr. Ann Graybiel's research on habit formation reveals why: our brains are efficiency machines, defaulting to established neural pathways under pressure. A two-day workshop can't rewire patterns that took years to form.

The companies seeing lasting change understand three things:
• Repetition beats revelation
• Environment shapes behavior more than intention
• Small, consistent practice trumps big, sporadic events

They design for reinforcement, not just education. They change systems, not just skills. They measure behavior adoption, not just training completion.

The gap between knowing and doing isn't a knowledge problem—it's a reinforcement problem.

What's your organization doing to bridge that gap?
