## Post Content

87% of sales training content is forgotten within 30 days.

Most organizations respond by adding more training sessions, more content, more workshops. They're solving for volume when the problem is retention.

Neuroscience reveals the gap: Our brains are wired to forget what we don't repeatedly use. The forgetting curve is steep, predictable, and unforgiving.

But here's what changes everything—spaced repetition with contextual practice.

Instead of cramming 8 hours of methodology into a workshop, deliver 15 minutes of reinforcement every week. Instead of teaching objection handling in isolation, trigger practice moments right after real objections occur.

You don't train harder to remember longer. You remember longer by training smarter.

The companies seeing sustainable behavior change aren't the ones with the biggest training budgets. They're the ones with the most systematic reinforcement engines.

What would change if your training stuck as well as it was delivered?
