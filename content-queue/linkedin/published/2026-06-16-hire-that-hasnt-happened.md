## Post Content

One rep who improves by 20% outperforms a hire you haven't made yet.

Pipeline demand is growing. The default answer is always the same.

"We need more reps."

But most teams have underperforming reps whose improvement would eliminate the need for that next hire.

Here's the math most leaders miss:

New hire: 5.7 months to ramp. $40K in recruiting costs. Full management overhead.

Existing rep: Knows your product. Has relationships. Understands the market.

The only thing missing? Consistent methodology execution under pressure.

That rep who skips the Pain Funnel when the deal gets tight. Who rushes the Up-Front Contract when the quarter-end pressure hits. Who qualifies budget with hope instead of rigor.

They're 80% there. The methodology training already happened.

But 80% execution compounds into 60% results over time.

A 15-20% improvement in their close rate compounds across their entire pipeline. No ramp time. No recruiting cost. No additional management bandwidth.

The most expensive rep on your team is the one who almost executes the methodology.

Because you keep hiring around the gap instead of closing it.
