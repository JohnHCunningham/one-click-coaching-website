## Post Content

87% of sales training content is forgotten within 30 days.

Yet most organizations keep investing in more content, more sessions, more training.

The neuroscience reveals why: our brains are wired to forget, not remember. Without reinforcement, synaptic connections weaken. Without application, knowledge becomes noise. Without repetition, insights fade to irrelevance.

The companies seeing lasting behavior change understand this. They don't focus on content delivery—they focus on content retention. They don't measure training completion—they measure skill application. They don't pile on more knowledge—they reinforce existing learning.

The gap isn't in what your salespeople learn. It's in what they retain, apply, and sustain.

Effective sales enablement is less about teaching and more about rewiring. Less about information transfer and more about habit formation. Less about one-time events and more about ongoing reinforcement.

What would change if you treated training like muscle memory instead of classroom education?
