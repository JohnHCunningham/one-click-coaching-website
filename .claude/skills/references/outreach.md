# Outreach Strategy — One Click Coaching

**Purpose:** Generate qualified discovery calls through cold outreach, LinkedIn engagement, and email campaigns.

**Principle:** Insight over pitch. Pattern recognition over persuasion. Restraint over volume.

---

## Cold Email Framework

### The OCC Email Structure

**Subject line → Hook → Problem → Insight → Soft ask**

Every email follows this architecture. No exceptions.

### Rhetorical Devices Requirement

**Every cold email must include 2-4 rhetorical devices.** This creates memorable, persuasive copy that stands out from generic outreach.

**The 6 Most Effective Devices for Cold Emails:**

1. **Antithesis** (Contrasting ideas)
   - "You installed Sandler. Your reps forgot it."
   - "Training teaches knowledge. Execution requires reinforcement."
   - Use in: Problem statement, insight section

2. **Tricolon** (Rule of three)
   - "Trained well. Executed poorly. ROI evaporated."
   - "Not because they didn't care. Not because training was bad. Because reinforcement never came."
   - Use in: Problem agitation, insight delivery

3. **Anaphora** (Repetition at beginning)
   - "Your reps know the methodology. Your reps believe in it. Your reps just don't execute it under pressure."
   - Use in: Building momentum, emphasizing pattern

4. **Metaphor** (Direct comparison, no "like")
   - "Your CRM is a graveyard of good intentions."
   - "Pressure is the X-ray of sales training."
   - Use in: Making abstract concepts vivid

5. **Rhetorical Question** (Use sparingly, never in subject line)
   - "What happened between the workshop and Monday morning?"
   - Use in: Transition to insight

6. **Anadiplosis** (End of one sentence becomes beginning of next)
   - "Training creates knowledge. Knowledge without reinforcement becomes forgotten theory."
   - Use in: Bridging ideas, creating flow

**Minimum:** 2 devices per email
**Optimal:** 3-4 devices per email
**Maximum:** 5 (beyond this feels forced)

### Subject Line Rules

**Length:** 3-7 words maximum
**Style:** Curiosity through restraint. Tension-based. No hype.

**The 4 Subject Line Formulas:**

1. **The Number**
   - "72 hours."
   - "37 calls. One pattern."
   - "$200k. No ROI."

2. **The Incomplete Statement**
   - "Your Sandler training has a half-life."
   - "The gap between training and Monday morning."
   - "Pressure reveals habit, not intention."

3. **The Provocation**
   - "More coaching isn't the problem."
   - "Your CRM isn't a coaching tool."
   - "Gong tells managers. Who tells reps?"

4. **The Question (Use Sparingly)**
   - "What happened after the Sandler rollout?"
   - "Where did the training ROI go?"

**Never:**
- Exclamation marks
- "Quick question"
- "Following up"
- "Just checking in"
- All caps
- Emojis

## Icebreaker System (Personalization Protocol)

**Rule:** Never send a template without personalization. Generic = ignored.

**The 3-Level Icebreaker Hierarchy:**

### Level 1: Deep Research (15-20 min per prospect)
**When to use:** High-value targets (VP of Sales at $50M+ company, Sandler franchisee, warm intro)

**LinkedIn Profile Personalization Protocol (Step-by-Step):**

1. **Headline Analysis** (30 sec)
   - Note their title and how they describe themselves
   - Look for methodology mentions (Sandler, MEDDIC, etc.)
   - Spot pain signals ("scaling," "building," "transforming")

2. **Recent Activity** (3-5 min)
   - Read last 5 posts (sort by Recent)
   - Look for: methodology references, training mentions, team challenges
   - Screenshot specific quote to reference in email
   - Note engagement level (high engagement = influencer status)

3. **About Section** (2 min)
   - Career trajectory (promoted recently? New role?)
   - Mission statements ("I help sales teams...")
   - Pain points embedded in bio

4. **Experience Section** (2 min)
   - Company tenure (< 6 months = settling in, good timing)
   - Previous roles (patterns of what they care about)
   - Methodology training certifications (Sandler badge, etc.)

5. **Company News Integration** (5-10 min)
   - **LinkedIn Company Page:** Recent posts, announcements
   - **Crunchbase:** Funding rounds (just raised = budget available)
   - **Google News:** Search "[Company Name] sales" for press releases
   - **Their Website:** Check for: new product launches, hiring (Sales Reps = growth)
   - **Job Postings:** Look for sales training keywords in LinkedIn jobs

**Personalization Formula:**
```
[LinkedIn insight] + [Company news] + [How OCC solves their specific pain]
```

**Example:**
> "I saw your post last week about onboarding strong but new reps backsliding after 90 days. You wrote: 'They know the playbook. They just don't run it under pressure.' I also noticed {{Company}} just raised Series B and is hiring 10 reps — exactly when methodology drift hits hardest. That's the problem One Click Coaching solves."

**Tools for Company News:**
- LinkedIn Company Page (free)
- Crunchbase (free tier shows recent funding)
- Google Alerts for "[Company Name] + sales" (free)
- BuiltWith (shows tech stack - are they using Gong/HubSpot?)

**What to reference:**
- Recent LinkedIn post (quote specific line with device - antithesis, metaphor)
- Podcast appearance (mention specific insight they shared)
- Blog post they wrote (tie OCC to their thesis)
- Company announcement (funding, product launch, training rollout)
- Mutual connection (not "we have a mutual connection" — name them and the context)
- Hiring signals (10 new sales roles = growth pain)

### Level 2: Medium Research (5-10 min per prospect)
**When to use:** Mid-tier targets (Sales Director, Enablement Manager, qualified ICP)

**What to reference:**
- LinkedIn headline or About section
- Recent job change (congratulate, then tie to OCC use case)
- Company size/industry (show you understand their context)
- Tools they use (visible in LinkedIn profile or company tech stack)

**Example:**
> "I see you're leading sales enablement at {{Company}} — mid-market SaaS, 15-20 reps. That's exactly the profile where methodology drift hits hardest. Your reps are trained, but manual coaching doesn't scale at that size."

### Level 3: Light Research (2-3 min per prospect)
**When to use:** Volume outreach (ICP match but lower priority, testing new segments)

**What to reference:**
- Industry pain (generic but relevant)
- Recent company news (funding, leadership change)
- Methodology training visible on company website

**Example:**
> "Most B2B SaaS companies invest heavily in sales training but struggle with reinforcement. I'm guessing {{Company}} has felt that pain too."

**Minimum bar:** At least Level 3 personalization. Never send fully generic.

**Template:**

**Or:**
> "{{Mutual connection}} suggested we connect."

**Never:**
- "I'd love to connect"
- "Let's grow our networks"
- Generic requests with no context

**Template:**
```
{{FirstName}},

Thanks for connecting. I saw your post about {{specific pain}}.

That's the exact problem One Click Coaching solves — we score every call against your methodology and fire same-day feedback to reps so training actually sticks.

Not pitching. Just thought it might be relevant given what you're working on.

If you're curious, happy to share what the scoring output looks like. 20 minutes, no pressure.
```

**Key principle:** Soft ask. Never "book a demo." Always "happy to show you."

## Email Sequence Structure

### Sequence 1: Cold Outreach (5 Emails Over 14 Days)

**Email 1 (Day 0):** Core insight email (Template 1, 2, 3, or 4)
- **Goal:** Get reply or meeting booked
- **CTA:** "Worth 20 minutes to see what this looks like?"

**Email 2 (Day 3):** Pattern recognition follow-up
- **Goal:** Reframe the problem from a different angle
- **Template:**
```
{{FirstName}},

Following up on my note from Tuesday.

Here's the pattern I see across every sales team we work with:
- Reps are trained on the methodology
- Managers don't have time to coach every call
- Performance plateaus 90 days after training

One Click Coaching closes that gap. Same-day feedback, every rep, every call.

Want to see what this looks like in practice?
```

**Email 3 (Day 7):** Social proof + outcome
- **Goal:** Build credibility with evidence
- **Template:**
```
{{FirstName}},

We analyzed 10,000 sales calls and found that 87% of methodology training is forgotten within 30 days without same-day reinforcement.

That's not a motivation problem. It's a system problem.

One Click Coaching is the system. Worth 20 minutes?
```

**Email 4 (Day 10):** Contrarian take
- **Goal:** Challenge assumptions, create cognitive dissonance
- **Template:**
```
{{FirstName}},

Most sales leaders think the solution to inconsistent performance is more training.

It's not. It's reinforcement.

Your reps already know what to do. They just don't do it under pressure. One Click Coaching reinforces the methodology at the moment it matters — the same day the call happened.

Curious if this resonates?
```

**Email 5 (Day 14):** Break-up email
- **Goal:** Create urgency through scarcity
- **Template:**
```
{{FirstName}},

I'll assume this isn't a priority right now and stop reaching out.

If that changes — or if you're just heads-down and want to revisit later — happy to show you what One Click Coaching looks like in practice.

Either way, good luck with the team.
```

**Key principle:** If no reply after Email 5, stop. Do not become spam.

## Human Voice Quality Control for Cold Emails (Target: 9.5/10)

**Cold emails get 3 seconds. If it sounds like AI, it's deleted.**

### Email-Specific Human Voice Rules

**✅ DO (Sounds Like a Real Person Wrote It):**

1. **Write Like You're Emailing a Colleague**
   - Use contractions: "I saw your post" not "I have observed"
   - Start with their name only: "{{FirstName}}," not "Dear {{FirstName}},"
   - Sign off simply: "{{YourName}}" not "Sincerely, {{Full Name and Title}}"

2. **Keep Sentences Short and Varied**
   - Some one-worders: "Gone." "Exactly." "Here's why."
   - Mix lengths: Long explanation, short punch, medium flow
   - Real emails have rhythm, not uniformity

3. **Be Blunt, Not Polished**
   - "Your training ROI evaporated" not "Your return on investment has diminished"
   - "That's $250k lost" not "This represents significant financial impact"
   - Humans are direct. AI is diplomatic.

4. **Use Numbers, Not Ranges**
   - "$15k per rep" not "significant investment per representative"
   - "90 days later" not "within the first quarter"
   - "12 hours/week" not "considerable weekly time"
   - Specificity = credibility = human

5. **Reference Real Things**
   - "I saw your post about onboarding challenges"
   - "I noticed {{Company}} just raised Series B"
   - "Your About section mentions Sandler"
   - Proves you did research (humans research, AI guesses)

---

### ❌ NEVER Use These in Cold Emails (AI Tells)

**Instant delete triggers:**

1. **Generic Openers:**
   - ❌ "I hope this email finds you well"
   - ❌ "I wanted to reach out"
   - ❌ "I came across your profile"
   - ❌ "I hope you don't mind me reaching out"
   - ✅ Start with their name and a specific insight

2. **Weak Closings:**
   - ❌ "I look forward to hearing from you"
   - ❌ "Please let me know if you'd like to discuss further"
   - ❌ "I'd love to connect at your convenience"
   - ✅ Direct ask: "Worth 20 minutes to see what this looks like?"

3. **Corporate Jargon:**
   - ❌ "Leverage synergies"
   - ❌ "Value proposition"
   - ❌ "Touch base"
   - ❌ "Circle back"
   - ❌ "Deep dive"
   - ✅ Talk like a human: "Here's how it works"

4. **Apologies:**
   - ❌ "Sorry to bother you"
   - ❌ "I know you're busy, but"
   - ❌ "Apologies for the unsolicited email"
   - ✅ No apologies. If you don't believe it's valuable, don't send it.

5. **Flattery:**
   - ❌ "I'm a huge fan of your work"
   - ❌ "Your impressive background"
   - ❌ "I've been following your career"
   - ✅ Skip flattery. Get to insight.

### The 3-Second Test (Cold Email Version)

**First 3 lines determine if email is read or deleted.**

**Bad (AI-Generated Feel):**
```
Hi {{FirstName}},

I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to reach out because I came across your LinkedIn profile and was impressed by your experience in sales leadership.

I work with companies like yours to help improve sales training ROI...
```

**Why it fails:**
- Generic opener (hope this finds you well)
- No specific insight
- Flattery without substance
- Sounds like every other cold email

**Good (Human-Written Feel):**
```
{{FirstName}},

Your post last week about reps backsliding after training: "They know the playbook. They just don't run it under pressure."

That's exactly the problem One Click Coaching solves.
```

**Why it works:**
- Immediate value (references their content)
- Direct (no fluff)
- Specific quote (proves research)
- Conversational tone

### Subject Line Human Voice Test

**AI-sounding subject lines:**
- ❌ "Quick question about sales training"

- ❌ "Following up on LinkedIn"

**Human-sounding subject lines:**

### Body Copy: Line-by-Line Human Polish

**For each sentence, ask:**
1. Would I say this out loud? (If no → too formal)
2. Is this specific or vague? (Vague → add numbers/names)
3. Am I hedging? (Remove "perhaps," "potentially," "might")
4. Is this necessary? (If no → delete)

**Example Edit:**

**Before (AI voice):**
> "Many organizations invest significantly in sales methodology training, yet often struggle to see sustained results over time."

**After (Human voice):**
> "You spent $200k on Sandler. Ninety days later, your reps are pitching features."

**What changed:**
- "Organizations" → "You" (direct address)
- "Significantly" → "$200k" (specific number)
- "Struggle to see sustained results" → "pitching features" (concrete behavior)
- Removed hedging ("often," "over time")

---

### Rhetorical Devices Must Sound Natural

**Forced (sounds AI):**
> "In the realm of sales training, one might observe that education imparts knowledge, whereas practical application cultivates proficiency."

**Natural (sounds human):**
> "Training teaches knowledge. Execution builds skill."

**The difference:**
- Shorter words (teaches vs. imparts)
- Parallel structure (antithesis)
- Sounds like speech, not a thesis

**All devices must pass the read-aloud test.**

### Common Cold Email Mistakes That Scream "AI"

1. **Every sentence is the same length** (16-20 words each)
   - Fix: Add variety. Some 3-word sentences. Some 30-word builds.

2. **Perfect grammar, zero personality**
   - Fix: Break a rule. Start with "And." Use a fragment.

3. **Generic personalization** ("I saw you work in sales")
   - Fix: Quote their specific LinkedIn post. Mention their Series B.

4. **No rhetorical devices**
   - Fix: Add 2-4 devices. Make it memorable.

5. **Hedging language** ("I believe," "perhaps," "potentially")
