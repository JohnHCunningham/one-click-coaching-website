## Original Draft

A sales manager with 12 reps cannot be the memory system for the methodology.

Not because they do not care.

Because the math breaks.

Twelve reps.
Eight calls a week.
Dozens of moments where pressure takes over.

A skipped upfront contract.
A shallow pain question.
A rushed demo.
A next step that sounds clear but is not real.

The manager may hear one call and coach it well.

But next week, the same rep drifts in a slightly different way.
Another rep repeats a pattern from two weeks ago.
A third improves, then slides back when the deal gets tense.

That is not a discipline problem.

It is a memory problem.

Methodology reinforcement cannot depend on one person remembering every rep's last call, last coaching note, last weakness, and next correction.

Managers should coach.

But they should not have to be the whole operating system.

The bottleneck is not intent.
It is bandwidth.

## Advisor Pass

### What Is Working

- The central frame is strong: managers are being asked to serve as the memory system for the methodology.
- The post protects the manager from blame, which will resonate with leaders who know their managers are overloaded.
- The specific behaviors make the post feel grounded in real sales calls.
- The post creates category demand without premature product pitching.

### What Is Weak

- The opening is strong, but the second line can create more tension before explaining the math.
- The close is good but could land with more consequence.
- The post could more clearly connect the memory problem to pipeline confidence.

### What the Audience Will Care About

Sales leaders will care that this explains why good managers still miss drift. It gives them language for a problem they have felt but may not have named: coaching is not only about intent or skill; it is also about observation, memory, and follow-through across many reps.

### What Should Be Sharpened

- Make the first two lines more scroll-stopping.
- Keep the manager-protective tone.
- Add one line showing why this matters commercially: pipeline stages can look clean while behavior is decaying.
- End with a cleaner aphorism.

### Stronger Hook Options

1. Your sales manager is not lazy.
   Your coaching system is asking them to remember too much.

2. A sales manager with 12 reps cannot be the memory system for your methodology.
   That is where training drift begins.

3. The methodology does not disappear all at once.
   It disappears one forgotten coaching note at a time.

4. Most undercoaching is not a motivation problem.
   It is a memory problem.

### Stronger Central Argument

Manual coaching breaks when the manager has to observe every call, diagnose methodology drift, remember each rep's prior coaching, avoid repeating the same advice, and know the next correction. That is too much cognitive load for one person. The manager should coach judgment and context. The system should remember patterns.

### Stronger Close

Managers should coach the person.
The system should remember the pattern.

That is how training survives contact with live calls.
