# LinkedIn Draft — The Team Norm That Kills Methodology

Date: 2026-05-21
Status: draft
Pillar: Story + Insight
Target persona: VP Sales, Sales Managers, Sales Enablement leaders, CROs at B2B companies with 5–50 sales reps
Hook type: Scene opener
Round: 1 (names the problem, no product mention)

---

She came out of training sharp.

Upfront contracts on every call. Pain questions that went three layers deep. She was doing the work — exactly what the workshop taught.

Then she looked around.

Nobody else was.

The rest of the team had been through the same training. Same Sandler. Same certification. But on live calls, they'd drifted back to what felt easier. Rushed discovery. Shallow qualification. Demos that started before the buyer earned them.

And here is the thing nobody talks about:

She did not stop because the methodology stopped working.

She stopped because she was the only one using it.

When the team norm is to skip the steps, even the best-trained rep eventually conforms. Not because the training was weak. Not because she lacks discipline. But because nobody wants to be the person still following the playbook when everyone else has moved on.

Training drift is not just an individual problem.

It is a culture problem. And unless reinforcement hits the whole team — consistently, call after call — the culture wins every time.

## Suggested First Comment

Methodology drift is usually discussed as an individual memory problem — the forgetting curve, the workshop fade.

But the social force is stronger than either.

When the team norm says the methodology is optional, even the best-trained rep eventually conforms. The question for sales leaders is not just whether individual reps remember the training. It is what the team is actually tolerating on live calls.
